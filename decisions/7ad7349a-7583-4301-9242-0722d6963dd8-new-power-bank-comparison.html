Compare portable chargers for mobile devices

Choosing the right power bank is a critical decision that extends beyond simple battery capacity. In an era where smartphones, tablets, and even laptops are essential for work, travel, and daily life, a reliable portable charger is a necessity, not a luxury. The ideal choice balances raw power, charging speed, and portability to match specific user needs, whether that's fast-charging a laptop on the go or ensuring a phone stays alive through a long trip. This comparison evaluates four high-performance power banks to identify the best overall option for most users.

The analysis reveals the Anker 737 Power Bank (PowerCore 24K) as the top performer, earning the highest overall score. It distinguishes itself with a rare combination of elite 140W charging speed, substantial 24,000mAh capacity, and premium build quality, making it a powerhouse for demanding users. For those prioritizing different factors, such as maximum capacity per dollar or unique design, compelling alternatives exist. The following comprehensive breakdown details how each model scored across key criteria to guide the purchasing decision.

Methodology: How the Power Banks Were Evaluated

The power banks in this comparison were assessed using a weighted scoring system across six critical criteria. Each criterion was assigned a weight reflecting its importance in the overall decision, with Capacity (2) and Charging Speed (1.8) being the most significant. Each product received a score from 1 to 5 for each criterion. The final weighted score was calculated by multiplying the raw score by the criterion’s weight and summing the results, providing a balanced, quantitative measure of overall performance that goes beyond subjective impressions.

Understanding the Evaluation Criteria

Before diving into the individual products, it’s essential to understand what each evaluation criterion means for the end-user experience.

  • Capacity (Weight: 2): Measured in milliampere-hours (mAh), this indicates the total energy stored. A higher mAh rating means more potential charges for your devices before the power bank itself needs recharging. This is the heaviest-weighted criterion as it defines the core utility of the product—how long it can keep your gadgets running.
  • Charging Speed (Weight: 1.8): This evaluates the maximum power output (in watts) and support for fast-charging protocols like Power Delivery (PD) and Quick Charge (QC). Higher wattage and broader protocol support translate to significantly faster charging times for compatible devices, especially important for laptops and tablets.
  • Port Selection (Weight: 1.6): The variety and type of ports (USB-C, USB-A, wireless charging pads) determine how many and what kinds of devices can be charged simultaneously. Versatility here adds convenience and future-proofs the investment.
  • Portability (Weight: 1.5): This considers the physical dimensions and weight. A power bank must be practical to carry; a high-capacity bank is less useful if it’s too bulky to take on a day trip.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.4): Encompassing materials, durability, and integrated safety features (overcharge, short-circuit, and temperature protection), this criterion impacts the product's longevity and safety during use.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): This assesses the price relative to the offered features, capacity, and speed. The goal is to identify which product delivers the most performance and utility for its cost.

Head-to-Head Power Bank Analysis

This section provides a detailed examination of each contender, breaking down its performance across the six key criteria.

Anker 737 Power Bank (PowerCore 24K)

The Anker 737 stands as a premium, high-performance portable charger designed for users with demanding power needs, from content creators to business travelers.

  • Capacity (5/5): With a 24,000mAh battery, it sits at the high end of consumer power banks, capable of charging a smartphone 4-5 times or providing 1-2 full charges to a laptop. Its standout feature is the integrated digital display, which provides precise, real-time information on remaining capacity and input/output wattage, eliminating guesswork.
  • Charging Speed (5/5): Its 140W maximum output is exceptional, enabling rapid charging for power-hungry devices like high-end laptops. Utilizing GaNPrime technology, it supports the latest PD 3.1 and QC protocols, making it one of the fastest charging solutions available in a portable form factor. It can efficiently charge three devices at once without significant speed drops.
  • Port Selection (4/5): It offers two USB-C ports and one USB-A port, covering most modern and legacy cable needs. The deduction comes from the lack of wireless charging, a feature sometimes expected at this price point.
  • Portability (2/5): This is its main trade-off. The high-capacity, high-power components result in a unit that is heavy (approximately 1.5 lbs) and bulky. It is not designed for pockets but is best carried in a backpack or travel bag.
  • Build Quality (4/5): Anker’s reputation for reliability is evident, with solid construction and comprehensive safety certifications. The display, while useful, can be prone to scratches.
  • Value for Money (3/5): Priced at $129.99, it commands a premium. The value is justified for professionals who need its extreme speed and capacity, but it may be overkill and over-budget for casual users.

INIU 65W 25000mAh Power Bank

The INIU power bank is a capacity champion focused on delivering maximum stored power and strong charging speed at a highly competitive price.

  • Capacity (5/5): At 25,000mAh, it offers the highest capacity in this comparison, capable of 4-6 smartphone charges. This makes it an excellent choice for extended trips or for users who need to keep multiple devices powered over several days.
  • Charging Speed (4/5): Its 65W PD output is robust and sufficient to fast-charge most laptops and phones effectively. While not the absolute fastest available, it handles demanding scenarios well and includes broad compatibility with various fast-charging protocols.
  • Port Selection (3/5): The triple output ports (mix of USB-C and USB-A) allow for charging multiple devices. It loses points for lacking wireless charging and not having a more diverse port array.
  • Portability (2/5): Similar to the Anker, the high 25,000mAh capacity results in significant size and weight, making it a bag-only companion rather than a pocketable item.
  • Build Quality (4/5): INIU provides reliable construction with essential safety features. While durable for everyday use, the plastic casing may not feel as premium as some competitors.
  • Value for Money (4/5): Priced at $59.99, it represents strong value, offering top-tier capacity and solid 65W charging for notably less than the premium options. The inclusion of a USB-C cable adds to its out-of-the-box value.

Shargeek Storm2 Slim 100W Power Bank

The Shargeek Storm2 Slim is a statement piece that prioritizes cutting-edge design and a high-tech aesthetic alongside powerful charging capabilities.

  • Capacity (3/5): With a 10,000mAh capacity, it provides about 2-3 smartphone charges. This is adequate for daily use but falls short compared to competitors offering double or triple the capacity at similar or lower price points, making it less ideal for multi-day travel.
  • Charging Speed (5/5): Its 100W output is superb, enabling very fast laptop and phone charging. It supports a wide array of protocols (PD 3.0, QC 4.0+, PPS), ensuring compatibility with a vast range of devices.
  • Port Selection (4/5): It provides two USB-C and one USB-A port, matching the connectivity of the Anker 737. The lack of wireless charging is again a minor drawback.
  • Portability (2/5): Although marketed as "Slim," the transparent shell and vibrant data display add bulk. It is less portable than minimalist designs focused purely on compactness.
  • Build Quality (4/5): The transparent design with visible internal components suggests a premium build. It includes necessary safety features, though the exposed aesthetic could make it more susceptible to cosmetic or impact damage.
  • Value for Money (2/5): At $149.99, it is the most expensive option here while offering the least capacity. The price heavily reflects the unique design and display, positioning it as a niche product for users who prioritize aesthetics and tech appeal above raw specifications.

Baseus 65W 20000mAh Power Bank

The Baseus model is a compact and convenient solution that emphasizes ease of recharging with its integrated design, offering a great balance of performance and price.

  • Capacity (4/5): The 20,000mAh battery is a strong middle-ground, providing 4-5 smartphone charges. It offers ample power for most users without the extreme bulk of the highest-capacity models.
  • Charging Speed (5/5): Delivering 65W of power, it matches the INIU’s fast-charging capability, making it fully capable of charging laptops and multiple devices quickly. Support for PD and QC ensures wide device compatibility.
  • Port Selection (3/5): It typically includes USB-C and USB-A ports. Its defining feature is the built-in, foldable AC plug, which eliminates the need to carry a separate charging cable. It does not include wireless charging.
  • Portability (2/5): While relatively compact for its capacity, any 20,000mAh power bank adds noticeable weight and bulk to a bag. The integrated plug improves convenience but not necessarily the physical footprint.
  • Build Quality (4/5): Baseus offers solid construction with standard safety protections. The foldable plug is convenient, though some users may question its long-term mechanical durability compared to a standard port.
  • Value for Money (3/5): At $49.99, it is the most affordable option in this comparison. It presents excellent value for users seeking 65W charging and good capacity, though those who don’t need laptop-fast charging may find even cheaper alternatives.

Final Verdict and Recommendations

Based on the weighted evaluation, the Anker 737 Power Bank (PowerCore 24K) emerges as the top recommendation for users who need uncompromising performance. Its winning combination of elite 140W charging speed, high 24,000mAh capacity, and the utility of its digital display justifies its premium price for power users, professionals, and tech enthusiasts who rely on their devices for demanding tasks.

However, the best choice ultimately depends on individual priorities:

  • Choose the Anker 737 if: You need the fastest possible charging for a laptop and multiple devices, value precise battery monitoring, and are willing to pay a premium for top-tier performance and build quality.
  • Choose the INIU 65W if: Maximum capacity and the best value per dollar are your primary goals. It’s ideal for long trips, camping, or as a shared power source where total available charges is more important than cutting-edge speed.
  • Choose the Shargeek Storm2 Slim if: Design and tech aesthetics are paramount. You want a conversation-starting piece with a cool display and very fast 100W charging, and you accept the trade-off of lower capacity for the style.
  • Choose the Baseus 65W if: Convenience and portability are key. The built-in AC plug makes recharging effortless, and it offers an excellent balance of speed, capacity, and price for everyday carry and travel.
