New Vacuum Cleaner Comparison

Published on January 24, 2026

Compare vacuums for your cleaning needs

Choosing the right vacuum cleaner involves balancing performance, features, and budget. This comparison evaluates four top contenders across critical factors like suction power, filtration, and value to determine the best overall option for most cleaning needs. The Dyson V15 Detect emerges as the top performer, achieving the highest weighted score due to its exceptional cleaning power and advanced filtration. However, the "best" vacuum depends heavily on individual priorities, whether that's budget-friendly pet hair cleaning, cordless convenience, or fully automated maintenance. This analysis breaks down how each model scored in key categories to help identify the ideal match for different homes and lifestyles.

The evaluation was conducted using a weighted scoring methodology. Six key criteria—Suction Power, Filtration, Versatility, Battery Life, Weight & Maneuverability, and Value for Money—were assigned weights based on their relative importance to the overall vacuum cleaner decision. Each product was scored out of 5 for each criterion, and the scores were multiplied by the criterion's weight to calculate a total weighted score, enabling a direct, objective comparison.

Understanding the Evaluation Criteria

Before diving into the individual vacuums, it’s essential to understand what each criterion measures and why it matters for your cleaning routine.

  • Suction Power (Weight: 2): This is the most heavily weighted factor, representing the vacuum's fundamental cleaning effectiveness. It assesses how well the machine removes dirt, debris, and pet hair from various surfaces, including deep-pile carpets, hard floors, and embedded dust. High suction is crucial for a thorough clean.
  • Filtration (Weight: 1.7): Critical for allergy sufferers and maintaining indoor air quality, filtration evaluates the system's ability to capture and retain microscopic particles like pollen, dust mites, and pet dander. A true sealed HEPA system is the gold standard, preventing expelled air from recirculating allergens.
  • Versatility (Weight: 1.6): This measures how well a vacuum adapts to different cleaning tasks. Key considerations include the quality and range of attachments for upholstery, stairs, and tight spaces, as well as its performance across both carpets and hard floors. Effectiveness on pet hair is a major component.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.5): For cordless models, runtime dictates cleaning freedom. This score reflects how long the vacuum can operate on a single charge, especially on higher power settings needed for carpets. A short runtime can be a significant limitation in larger homes.
  • Weight & Maneuverability (Weight: 1.4): Ease of use is paramount. This criterion considers the vacuum's physical weight, balance, and steering capability. A lightweight, well-maneuverable model is easier to carry up stairs, push under furniture, and use for extended periods.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): This balances the purchase price against the performance, features, and build quality offered. A high score indicates strong performance per dollar, while a low score suggests a premium price that may not be justified for all users.

Head-to-Head Vacuum Cleaner Comparison

This section provides a detailed analysis of each vacuum cleaner, examining its performance across the six weighted criteria.

Dyson V15 Detect Cordless Vacuum Cleaner

The Dyson V15 Detect is a premium cordless stick vacuum that combines innovative technology with powerful cleaning. It scored 38.0 points, claiming the top spot.

  • Performance Analysis: The Dyson excels in the highest-weighted categories. Its Suction Power (5/5) is driven by a Hyperdymium motor and enhanced by a unique laser dust detection system that reveals hidden debris on hard floors. Its Filtration (5/5) is a fully sealed HEPA system, capturing 99.97% of particles as small as 0.3 microns, making it outstanding for allergy sufferers. Versatility (4/5) is strong with included tools for various surfaces and anti-tangle hair removal, though some niche attachments are sold separately.
  • Trade-offs: The primary compromises come in Battery Life (3/5), which drops to 20-30 minutes on max power, and Value for Money (2/5) due to its premium $749 price tag. Its Weight & Maneuverability (4/5) is generally good but can feel top-heavy with attachments.
  • Best For: Users who prioritize the deepest clean, best-in-class filtration, and cutting-edge technology, and who have a budget to match.

Shark Vertex Pro Cordless Vacuum with DuoClean PowerFins

The Shark Vertex Pro is a powerful cordless competitor that offers high-end features at a more moderate price point of $449, scoring 34.6 points for second place.

  • Performance Analysis: The Shark matches the Dyson in Suction Power (5/5), thanks to its effective DuoClean PowerFins and a self-cleaning brushroll that proactively prevents hair wrap. It is highly Versatile (4/5), transitioning well between surfaces and including LED headlights. Its Weight & Maneuverability (4/5) is also praised for easy handling.
  • Trade-offs: The key differentiators where it trails the Dyson are Filtration (3/5), as its HEPA system isn't fully sealed against leakage, and Battery Life (2/5), which offers only about 30 minutes of high-power runtime. Its Value for Money (3/5) is middling; it's less expensive than the Dyson but faces stronger value competition.
  • Best For: Homeowners seeking powerful cordless suction and excellent pet hair management without the absolute highest price, and who may have fewer allergy concerns.

iRobot Roomba j7+ Self-Emptying Robot Vacuum

The iRobot Roomba j7+ represents a different approach: automated, hands-free cleaning. Priced at $799, it scored 33.5 points, emphasizing convenience over raw power.

  • Performance Analysis: This robot vacuum scores highest in Weight & Maneuverability (5/5)—it requires no user effort at all, navigating and avoiding obstacles autonomously. Its Battery Life (4/5) is efficient, with 90-minute runtimes and automatic recharging/resume. Filtration (4/5) is also very good for a robot model.
  • Trade-offs: By design, it lacks Versatility (2/5), as it cannot clean stairs, upholstery, or above-floor areas. Its Suction Power (3/5) is sufficient for hard floors and low-pile carpets but can't match stick vacuums on deep carpets. The high initial investment results in an average Value for Money (3/5) score, as it's a supplement rather than a complete replacement for many households.
  • Best For: Individuals or families who want daily maintenance cleaning of floors without any manual intervention, and who can complement it with a manual vacuum for deeper, whole-home cleans.

Bissell CleanView Swivel Pet Upright Vacuum

The Bissell CleanView Pet is a budget-friendly, corded upright designed specifically for pet owners. At just $169, it scored 27.5 points, highlighting a classic value proposition.

  • Performance Analysis: This model shines in Value for Money (4/5), delivering reliable core cleaning at an accessible price. It is also highly Versatile (4/5) for pet owners, coming with specialized tools for hair removal. Its Suction Power (3/5) is adequate for most tasks.
  • Trade-offs: As a corded upright, it automatically scores lowest in Battery Life (1/5), being tethered to an outlet. Its Filtration (2/5) lacks a true HEPA filter, making it less suitable for severe allergies. Its Weight & Maneuverability (3/5) is acceptable on one level but cumbersome to carry between floors.
  • Best For: Budget-conscious pet owners in smaller homes or apartments who prioritize pet hair cleaning tools and don't mind the limitations of a corded, upright design.

Verdict and Final Recommendations

The Dyson V15 Detect Cordless Vacuum Cleaner is the recommended winner for users seeking the most powerful and technologically advanced cleaning solution. It achieved the highest total score by dominating the two most important criteria: Suction Power and Filtration. For homes where cleaning effectiveness and allergen control are the top priorities, and where the premium cost is justifiable, the Dyson V15 provides a top-tier experience.

However, the "best" vacuum is context-dependent. Consider these alternative recommendations based on specific needs:

  • Choose the Shark Vertex Pro if you want near-Dyson suction power and excellent pet hair tools at a significantly lower price, and can accept shorter battery life and good-but-not-elite filtration.
  • Choose the iRobot Roomba j7+ if your primary goal is daily, hands-off floor maintenance and you are willing to invest in convenience automation, understanding it will need to be paired with a manual vacuum for whole-home deep cleans.
  • Choose the Bissell CleanView Pet if your budget is tight, you have pets, and you need a reliable, no-frills workhorse for weekly cleaning sessions. Its corded design guarantees unlimited runtime.

Ultimately, aligning the vacuum's strengths—whether raw power, smart features, automation, or pure value—with your specific home layout, surface types, and cleaning priorities is the key to a satisfying purchase.
