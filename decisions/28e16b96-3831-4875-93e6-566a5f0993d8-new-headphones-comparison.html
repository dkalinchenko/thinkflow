New Headphones Comparison

Published on January 24, 2026

Compare wireless headphones and earbuds

Choosing the right pair of wireless headphones is a balancing act between performance, comfort, and price. For users seeking an immersive listening experience, features like active noise cancellation (ANC), long battery life, and high-quality audio are paramount, yet finding a model that excels in all areas without breaking the bank can be challenging. This comparison analyzes four popular over-ear headphones in the budget-to-mid-range segment: the Sony WH-CH720N, Anker Soundcore Space Q45, JBL Tune 770NC, and Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT. After a detailed evaluation across six weighted criteria, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 emerges as the overall winner. It achieves this by offering an exceptional blend of class-leading battery life, strong sound quality, and effective noise cancellation at a highly competitive price point, presenting the best overall value. For users with different priorities, such as maximum comfort or pure sound accuracy, compelling alternatives exist.

The evaluation of these headphones was conducted using a weighted scoring system. Six key criteria—Sound Quality, Noise Cancellation, Comfort, Battery Life, Build Quality, and Value for Money—were assigned importance weights based on their typical significance to the average buyer. Each headphone was scored out of 5 for each criterion. These raw scores were then multiplied by their respective weights and summed to produce a total weighted score, allowing for a nuanced comparison that reflects real-world priorities.

Understanding the Evaluation Criteria

When comparing headphones, it's essential to know what each performance category means for the user experience.

  • Sound Quality (Weight: 2): This is the most critical factor, assessing the clarity, balance, and richness of audio. It considers whether bass, mids, and treble are well-represented without one overpowering the others, and how suitable the sound profile is for different music genres and content types.
  • Noise Cancellation (Weight: 1.8): Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) uses microphones and inverse sound waves to reduce ambient noise. Effectiveness is measured by how well it dampens constant low-frequency sounds (like airplane engines) and sudden, higher-frequency noises (like chatter or keyboard clicks).
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.5): Critical for long listening sessions, comfort encompasses the weight of the headphones, the plushness and breathability of the earcup padding, and the clamping force of the headband. A poor fit can lead to listener fatigue.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.3): This measures the hours of continuous playback on a single charge, typically with ANC enabled. Long battery life enhances convenience and reduces charging anxiety, especially for travelers and daily commuters.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.2): This criterion evaluates the durability of materials (plastic, metal, leather) and the robustness of hinges and joints. It indicates how well the headphones will withstand regular use and potential travel stresses.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): Perhaps the ultimate deciding factor, this assesses the total package of features, performance, and build relative to the price point. It identifies which model delivers the most for the user's investment.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Sony WH-CH720N: The Comfort and Connectivity Champion

The Sony WH-CH720N excels in delivering a supremely comfortable user experience with reliable core features. Its standout attribute is its 5/5 Comfort score, achieved through an exceptionally lightweight design and well-padded earcups that minimize fatigue during extended use. It also boasts a 5/5 in Battery Life, offering a substantial 35 hours of playback with ANC active.

However, these strengths come with trade-offs. To achieve its light weight, Sony utilized a plastic build that scores a 2/5 for Build Quality, feeling less premium and somewhat creaky compared to rivals. Its Sound Quality (3/5) is clear but considered somewhat bass-light and lacks the detail of higher-end models. Its noise cancellation (4/5) is effective for constant background hum but less adept at cutting out irregular sounds.

Best For: Users who prioritize all-day comfort and seamless multi-device connectivity above absolute premium build or audiophile-grade sound.

Anker Soundcore Space Q45: The Balanced Value Leader

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 wins through remarkable balance and exceptional value. It earns a perfect 5/5 in Value for Money and Battery Life, the latter being arguably its killer feature with over 50 hours of playtime with ANC on. Its Sound Quality (4/5) is well-tuned and balanced, offering good detail and a customizable EQ via its companion app.

Its Noise Cancellation (3/5) is competent for the price but doesn't match premium sector leaders, and its Build Quality (3/5) is adequate but plasticky. Some users may also find its design slightly bulky. Despite these minor compromises, the Q45’s overall package—outstanding endurance, strong audio, and decent ANC at a $129 price point—makes it the top-ranked option with a total weighted score of 37.0.

Best For: The budget-conscious power user seeking the best combination of marathon battery life, good sound, and effective features without a premium price tag.

JBL Tune 770NC: The Bass-Heavy Endurance Pick

The JBL Tune 770NC is an affordable gateway to wireless ANC, retailing at just $99. Its most compelling feature is its enormous 70-hour battery life (scoring 4/5), which leads the group when ANC is off. It appeals to a specific listener with its bass-heavy sound signature, though this comes at the cost of muddier mids and treble, resulting in a 3/5 Sound Quality score for non-bass-focused genres.

Its Noise Cancellation (3/5) is functional but average, and its Build Quality (2/5) reflects its low price, with a plasticky feel. The comfort (4/5) is generally good due to its over-ear design, but the fit can feel bulky. It represents strong Value for Money (4/5) for those who prioritize long battery and powerful bass above all else.

Best For: Bass enthusiasts and budget-first buyers who want the longest possible battery life and are less concerned with nuanced sound or premium materials.

Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT: The Purist's Sound Choice

The Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT takes a different approach, forgoing ANC entirely to focus on sound accuracy and reliability. It shines with a 4/5 in Sound Quality, delivering the most balanced and clear frequency response of the group, true to Audio-Technica's studio heritage. It also features a very long 60-hour battery life (4/5).

The major compromise is the complete lack of Active Noise Cancellation (scoring 2/5 for the criterion), relying only on passive isolation from its earcups. Its Build (3/5) and Comfort (3/5) are utilitarian and geared toward stationary use rather than fashion or all-day wear. For the right user, it offers excellent Value (4/5) by delivering superb wireless audio for critical listening at a low cost.

Best For: Aspiring audio engineers, critical listeners, and purists who prioritize accurate sound reproduction over ANC and flashy features.

Verdict and Final Recommendations

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 is the recommended choice for most buyers seeking wireless over-ear headphones. It wins by mastering the fundamentals: it lasts dramatically longer than competitors, sounds great for its price, and includes effective noise cancellation, all while maintaining a very accessible price. It is the definition of a well-rounded performer.

However, the "best" headphone ultimately depends on individual priorities:

  • Choose the Sony WH-CH720N if your primary concern is feather-light comfort for all-day wear and you value strong brand integration and multi-point connectivity.
  • Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if you are on a strict budget, crave powerful bass, and need the absolute maximum battery life available.
  • Choose the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT if you are a critical listener, podcaster, or musician who values accurate sound above all else and does not require active noise cancellation.

Each of these models successfully carves out a niche in the competitive headphone market. By aligning a model's strengths with your personal weighting of features—be it battery, bass, balance, or budget—you can make a confident and satisfying choice.
