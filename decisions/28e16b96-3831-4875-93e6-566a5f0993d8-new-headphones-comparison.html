New Headphones Comparison

Published on January 24, 2026

Compare wireless headphones and earbuds

Selecting the right pair of wireless headphones involves balancing priorities like audio fidelity, noise control, comfort, and budget. With numerous models on the market, each promising a premium experience, the decision can be overwhelming. This comparison evaluates four popular models across six weighted criteria to identify the best overall value. The winner, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45, distinguishes itself by delivering exceptional battery life, strong sound quality, and effective noise cancellation at a competitive price point, making it a standout all-rounder. For those with different priorities—such as maximum comfort, studio-grade sound, or the lowest possible cost—other contenders in this lineup present compelling cases.

The evaluation uses a weighted scoring system to reflect the varying importance of each feature to the average buyer. Each headphone was rated on a scale of 1 to 5 for Sound Quality, Noise Cancellation, Comfort, Battery Life, Build Quality, and Value for Money. These scores were then multiplied by their respective weights (shown in parentheses next to each criterion below) to calculate a total score. This method ensures that the final ranking reflects a balanced perspective, where critical factors like audio performance and noise cancellation have a greater impact on the outcome than secondary considerations.

In-Depth Look at the Evaluation Criteria

Understanding why each criterion matters helps contextualize the scores and final recommendations.

  • Sound Quality (Weight: 2.0): The primary function of any headphone. This score assesses clarity, balance across bass, mids, and treble, and overall audio fidelity. A higher score indicates a more accurate, detailed, and enjoyable listening experience suitable for a wide range of music genres.
  • Noise Cancellation (Weight: 1.8): Critical for commuting, travel, or focus in noisy environments. This evaluates the effectiveness of Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) technology at reducing ambient sounds, from constant low-frequency hums to intermittent higher-frequency noises.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.5): Determines how long you can wear the headphones without fatigue. Factors include clamping force, weight, earcup padding material, and breathability. Superior comfort is essential for long work sessions, flights, or extended listening periods.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.3): Measures hours of playback on a single charge, impacting convenience and reliability. Longer battery life reduces charging frequency, making the headphones more suitable for travel and daily use without anxiety.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.2): An assessment of durability, material choice, and overall construction. This influences the product's longevity and its premium feel. Higher scores indicate robust designs that can withstand regular use.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): Perhaps the most holistic criterion, this judges the overall package relative to the asking price. It considers which features are included, their performance level, and where compromises are made to hit a specific price point.

Head-to-Head Wireless Headphones Analysis

Here is a detailed breakdown of each model, its performance across the key criteria, and who it's best suited for.

Sony WH-CH720N: The Lightweight Comfort King

The Sony WH-CH720N prioritizes comfort and core functionality, making it an excellent choice for users who wear headphones for hours on end.

Strengths & Performance Highlights:

  • Unmatched Comfort: With a perfect 5/5 score, its extremely lightweight design and well-padded earcups set a high bar for all-day wearability without causing fatigue.
  • Excellent Battery & Features: A 5/5 score in Battery Life reflects its impressive 35-hour runtime with ANC active. It also supports convenient features like multi-point Bluetooth connectivity.
  • Competent Noise Cancellation: Scoring 4/5, its ANC effectively tackles consistent background noises like office air conditioning or travel rumble, performing well for its class.

Weaknesses & Trade-offs:

  • Sound Quality Limitations: Its 3/5 score indicates clear but bass-light audio by default, with some compression at higher volumes, making it less engaging for bass-heavy genres.
  • Build Quality Compromise: To achieve its low weight, Sony used plastics that can feel creaky and less durable, resulting in a below-average 2/5 score for Build Quality.

Best For: Users who prioritize featherweight comfort and long battery life for work, study, or travel, and who are willing to accept a less premium build and a neutral-to-bright sound signature.

Check Price and Reviews: Sony WH-CH720N on Amazon

Anker Soundcore Space Q45: The Feature-Packed Value Champion

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45, our overall winner, achieves the highest total score by delivering a well-rounded, no-compromise experience at a mid-range price.

Strengths & Performance Highlights:

  • Exceptional Overall Value: A perfect 5/5 in Value for Money is earned by packing premium features like strong ANC, LDAC high-res audio support, and class-leading battery into an affordable package.
  • Benchmark Battery Life: Another 5/5 score, with over 50 hours of playtime with ANC on, it virtually eliminates daily charging anxiety and outperforms nearly all competitors.
  • Strong, Balanced Audio: Scoring 4/5 for Sound Quality, it delivers clear, well-tuned sound with a solid bass foundation and good detail, appealing to a broad audience.

Weaknesses & Trade-offs:

  • Noise Cancellation is Good, Not Elite: Its 3/5 ANC score means it handles constant noise well but isn't as effective as top-tier models against sudden, sharp sounds or high-frequency chatter.
  • Bulky Design: Some users may find the fit a bit large or the carrying case inconveniently big, though comfort remains high with a 4/5 score.

Best For: The majority of buyers seeking the best balance of battery, sound, features, and price. It’s the ideal all-rounder for everyday use, commuting, and travel.

Check Price and Reviews: Anker Soundcore Space Q45 on Amazon

JBL Tune 770NC: The Bass-Heavy Budget Option

The JBL Tune 770NC offers an enticing combination of a low price, massive battery life, and a fun sound profile favored by bass enthusiasts.

Strengths & Performance Highlights:

  • Incredible Battery Efficiency: With a 4/5 score, it boasts up to 70 hours of playback (without ANC), making it one of the longest-lasting options available.
  • Powerful, Energetic Sound: It delivers a pronounced bass response (3/5 score) that brings energy to pop, hip-hop, and electronic music, which many casual listeners prefer.
  • Strong Value Proposition: A 4/5 score for Value acknowledges its compelling mix of ANC, epic battery life, and foldable design at a very accessible price point.

Weaknesses & Trade-offs:

  • Sound Can Lack Clarity: The boosted bass can muddy the midrange and treble, reducing overall clarity and making it less ideal for acoustic, classical, or critical listening.
  • Average Build and ANC: Both Build Quality (2/5) and Noise Cancellation (3/5) are functional but unremarkable, representing clear areas of cost-saving.

Best For: Budget-conscious listeners who crave powerful bass and prioritize marathon battery life over nuanced sound accuracy or premium construction.

Check Price and Reviews: JBL Tune 770NC on Amazon

Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT: The Studio-Accurate Purist's Choice

The Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT takes a different approach, focusing on sound accuracy and simplicity by forgoing active noise cancellation entirely.

Strengths & Performance Highlights:

  • Excellent Sound Accuracy: Its 4/5 score in Sound Quality reflects a balanced, clear frequency response ideal for critical listening, mixing, and appreciating audio as it was intended.
  • Superb Battery Life: With 60 hours of playback, it earns a 4/5 score, providing exceptional reliability for a wired-first design.
  • Wired Versatility: It can be used as a passive wired headphone even with a dead battery, a unique advantage for professionals or as a backup.

Weaknesses & Trade-offs:

  • No Active Noise Cancellation: The lack of ANC results in a low 2/5 score in that category, limiting its usefulness in noisy environments.
  • Utilitarian Comfort & Design: With a 3/5 comfort score, its studio-oriented design has more clamp force and less plush padding than consumer-focused models.

Best For: Audiophiles, content creators, and students on a budget who prioritize accurate sound reproduction for study or creative work and do not require ANC.

Check Price and Reviews: Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT on Amazon

Final Verdict and Tailored Recommendations

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 emerges as the clear winner in this comparison. It successfully executes a "no weak links" strategy, delivering high performance in the most important areas—especially battery life and value—without a major failing in any single category. For the average buyer seeking wireless headphones that excel in everyday scenarios, the Space Q45 represents the most intelligent and satisfying investment.

However, the best choice ultimately depends on individual needs:

  • Choose the Sony WH-CH720N if your top priority is all-day, forget-you're-wearing-them comfort for work or extended sessions.
  • Choose the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 if you want the best overall package of battery, features, and sound for the money (the recommended choice for most).
  • Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if you are on a strict budget and love a powerful, bass-forward sound signature.
  • Choose the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT if you are an aspiring audio professional or purist who values sound accuracy above all else and does not need ANC.
