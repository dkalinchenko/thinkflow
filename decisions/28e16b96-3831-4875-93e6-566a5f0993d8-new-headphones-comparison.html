New Headphones Comparison

Published on January 24, 2026

Compare wireless headphones and earbuds

Choosing the right pair of wireless headphones involves balancing a mix of critical factors, from audio fidelity and noise-blocking prowess to all-day comfort and battery endurance. This comparison focuses on four popular models in the budget to mid-range segment: the Sony WH-CH720N, Anker Soundcore Space Q45, JBL Tune 770NC, and the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT. Each offers a distinct approach to wireless audio, catering to different listener priorities. After a detailed evaluation across six weighted criteria, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 emerges as the overall winner. It achieves this by offering a remarkable combination of best-in-class battery life, strong and customizable sound, effective noise cancellation, and high comfort, all at a highly competitive price point. For buyers seeking a feature-rich package without a premium price tag, the Space Q45 represents exceptional value. However, the ideal choice ultimately depends on whether a user’s priority is featherlight comfort, thunderous bass, or studio-accurate sound above all else.

This analysis used a weighted scoring system to reflect the varying importance of different features to the average buyer. Sound Quality and Noise Cancellation were given the highest emphasis, followed closely by Comfort and Value for Money. Battery Life and Build Quality were also significant factors. Each headphone was scored on a scale of 1 to 5 for each criterion, with those scores multiplied by the criterion's weight to calculate a total points value. This method ensures the final ranking reflects a balanced, prioritized view of performance rather than treating all features as equally important.

Evaluation Criteria: What Matters in Wireless Headphones?

Understanding the criteria used for evaluation provides context for the scores and rankings. Each factor plays a specific role in the overall user experience.

  • Sound Quality (Weight: 2.0): This is the core function of any audio device. It encompasses audio clarity, the balance between bass, midrange, and treble frequencies, and the richness of the soundstage. A high score indicates accurate, enjoyable audio suitable for a wide range of music genres.
  • Noise Cancellation (Weight: 1.8): Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) uses microphones and opposing sound waves to reduce ambient noise. Its effectiveness at silencing low-frequency rumbles (like plane engines) and higher-frequency chatter is crucial for travel, commuting, and focused listening.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.5): Headphones are often worn for hours. Comfort is determined by the weight, clamping force, quality of padding on the headband and ear cups, and overall fit. A comfortable pair can be worn all day without causing fatigue or pressure points.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.3): Measured in hours of playback, battery life dictates how often you need to recharge. Long battery life is essential for travelers, commuters, and anyone who wants to avoid daily charging anxiety.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.2): This assesses the durability of materials (plastic, metal, leather) and the robustness of the construction, including hinges and joints. Better build quality typically translates to a longer lifespan and a more premium feel.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): Perhaps the most holistic criterion, this evaluates the total package of features, performance, and build relative to the asking price. The best value doesn't necessarily mean the cheapest, but the one that offers the most satisfying experience for the cost.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Sony WH-CH720N: The Comfort & Connectivity Champion

Sony's WH-CH720N focuses on delivering a supremely lightweight and user-friendly experience with solid core features.

Strengths & Performance:
The standout feature is its exceptional comfort (5/5), stemming from an extremely lightweight design and ample padding that avoids excessive clamping pressure. It pairs this with outstanding battery life (5/5), offering up to 35 hours of playback even with ANC activated. Its noise cancellation (4/5) is effective for its price, adeptly handling constant low-frequency noises. The inclusion of multi-point Bluetooth is a significant convenience for users switching between devices.

Weaknesses & Trade-offs:
These advantages come with clear compromises. The build quality (2/5) is a notable weak point, with a plasticky construction that can feel creaky and less durable. The sound quality (3/5) is clear but somewhat bass-light and lacks the detail and richness found in some competitors, sounding slightly compressed at high volumes.

Bottom Line: The Sony WH-CH720N is the ideal choice for users who prioritize all-day comfort, long battery life, and seamless multi-device connectivity, and are willing to accept a less premium build and a competent but not exceptional sound signature.

Anker Soundcore Space Q45: The Feature-Rich Value Leader

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 wins this comparison by excelling in multiple categories without a significant weak spot, delivering a comprehensive package.

Strengths & Performance:
It sets a high bar with best-in-class battery life (5/5), boasting over 50 hours with ANC on—far surpassing most rivals. Its sound quality (4/5) is well-balanced and clear, with solid bass and good detail, and can be finely tuned via a companion app. Comfort (4/5) is high thanks to plush memory foam ear cups, and its value for money (5/5) is exceptional, packing features often found in more expensive models.

Weaknesses & Trade-offs:
The noise cancellation (3/5) is good but not class-leading, effectively reducing constant drone but struggling more with higher-frequency sounds. Some users may find the design slightly bulky, and the build quality (3/5), while adequate, uses plastics that don't feel as premium as the audio performance.

Bottom Line: The Space Q45 is the top recommendation for most buyers seeking the best overall blend of endurance, sound, ANC, and comfort for under $130. It is the definitive value champion.

JBL Tune 770NC: The Bass-Powered Budget Option

The JBL Tune 770NC caters to a specific listener: those who want powerful, energetic sound and marathon battery life on a tight budget.

Strengths & Performance:
It delivers an impressive 70-hour battery life (4/5) with ANC off, making it incredibly reliable for extended use. The sound profile emphasizes a powerful, deep bass response, which appeals to fans of EDM, hip-hop, and action movies. It is also comfortable (4/5) and includes a foldable design and an AUX cable for versatility.

Weaknesses & Trade-offs:
The sound quality (3/5) score reflects a bass-heavy signature that can overwhelm mids and treble, reducing clarity and making it less ideal for varied genres or critical listening. The noise cancellation (3/5) is merely average, and the build quality (2/5) feels notably plasticky and less robust than competitors.

Bottom Line: Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if your primary desires are earth-shaking bass and the absolute longest possible battery life for under $100, and you are comfortable with significant trade-offs in sound balance and build durability.

Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT: The Studio-Accurate Purist's Pick

The Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT takes a different path, forgoing popular features like ANC to focus on sound accuracy and reliable functionality.

Strengths & Performance:
It offers excellent, balanced sound quality (4/5) with clear mids and detailed treble, making it suitable for critical listening, mixing, and genres where accuracy is key. It also provides very long 60-hour battery life (4/5) and the unique ability to function in wired mode even with a dead battery, a boon for professionals.

Weaknesses & Trade-offs:
The most significant limitation is the complete lack of active noise cancellation (2/5), relying only on passive isolation. This makes it a poor choice for noisy environments. Its studio-focused design is utilitarian and may not appeal to all, and comfort (3/5) is adequate but not best-in-class for very long sessions.

Bottom Line: The ATH-M20xBT is the clear choice for budding audio engineers, podcasters, or listeners who prioritize accurate sound reproduction above all else and primarily use headphones in quiet environments. It is a tool, not a luxury gadget.

Verdict & Final Recommendations

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 is the recommended winner for the majority of users. It claims the top spot by delivering no major weaknesses and leading strengths in the most impactful areas: it has the best battery life in the group, offers great customizable sound and comfort, and packages it all at a price that represents outstanding value. It is the most well-rounded and reliable performer.

However, the "best" headphones are subjective and depend on your personal hierarchy of needs:

  • Choose the Sony WH-CH720N if: You wear headphones for 8+ hours at a time and value featherlight comfort and seamless switching between your phone and laptop above having the absolute best sound or most solid build.
  • Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if: You are on a strict budget, live for powerful bass, and need headphones that can last a full week of commuting without a recharge. Be prepared for a less refined sound profile.
  • Choose the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT if: You are an aspiring creator, a fan of acoustic or classical music, or simply want the most accurate and balanced sound in this price range, and you do not require active noise cancellation.
