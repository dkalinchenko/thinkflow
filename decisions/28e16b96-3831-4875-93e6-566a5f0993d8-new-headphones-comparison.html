Published on January 24, 2026

Compare wireless headphones and earbuds

Selecting the right pair of wireless headphones involves balancing several competing priorities: sound quality, noise cancellation, comfort, and value. With prices ranging from under $100 to just over $130, the four models compared here—the Sony WH-CH720N, Anker Soundcore Space Q45, JBL Tune 770NC, and Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT—represent compelling options in the budget to mid-range segment. Each takes a distinct approach, prioritizing different aspects of the listening experience. After a detailed evaluation against weighted criteria, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 emerges as the overall winner, offering the best balance of features, battery life, and sound for its price point. For users prioritizing supreme comfort and brand reliability, the Sony WH-CH720N is a close contender, while the JBL and Audio-Technica models serve specific listener profiles seeking powerful bass or studio-accurate sound, respectively.

Executive Summary

The search for the best wireless headphones under $130 reveals a competitive field where manufacturers make strategic trade-offs to hit a price point. This comparison evaluates four highly-rated models across six critical performance areas to determine which provides the best overall package for most users. The winner, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45, distinguishes itself with class-leading battery life, strong sound quality, and effective noise cancellation, delivering premium-adjacent features at a mid-range price. The Sony WH-CH720N follows closely, carving its niche with an exceptionally lightweight and comfortable design, making it ideal for all-day wear. The JBL Tune 770NC offers incredible battery life and a bass-forward sound signature for entertainment-focused listeners, and the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT appeals to purists who prioritize accurate sound reproduction and can forgo active noise cancellation. The final ranking reflects a nuanced picture where the best choice depends heavily on individual preferences and which features are deemed non-negotiable.

Methodology

This comparison employs a weighted scoring system to objectively evaluate each pair of headphones. Six key criteria—Sound Quality, Noise Cancellation, Comfort, Battery Life, Build Quality, and Value for Money—were assigned weights reflecting their relative importance to the overall listening experience and purchasing decision. Each product was scored on a scale of 1 to 5 for each criterion. The raw score was then multiplied by the criterion's weight to calculate a weighted score. The product with the highest total weighted score is declared the overall winner. This method ensures that critical factors like sound quality have a greater impact on the final outcome than less critical ones.

Understanding the Evaluation Criteria

Choosing headphones is personal, but focusing on the right technical and ergonomic factors leads to a more satisfying purchase.

  • Sound Quality (Weight: 2.0): The foremost job of any headphone. This score evaluates audio clarity, bass response, treble detail, and overall balance. A higher score indicates a more accurate, immersive, and pleasing sound across various music genres and content types.
  • Noise Cancellation (Weight: 1.8): Critical for travel, commuting, or focused work. This measures the effectiveness of Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) technology at reducing ambient noise like engine rumble, office chatter, and air conditioning. Better ANC creates a quieter, more isolated listening environment.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.5): Determines how long you can wear the headphones without fatigue. Factors include clamping force, weight distribution, earcup padding material, and breathability. High comfort is essential for long flights, work sessions, or extended gaming.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.3): Defines convenience and reliability. Measured in hours of playback, longer battery life reduces charging frequency and anxiety. Quick charge capabilities are a valuable bonus.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.2): An indicator of durability and material feel. This assesses the sturdiness of hinges, headband, and materials used (plastic, metal, leatherette). Better build quality suggests longer product lifespan and a more premium tactile experience.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): The ultimate measure of a purchase. This score judges how well the headphone's performance, features, and build justify its price tag. The best value doesn't always mean the cheapest, but rather the most features and performance per dollar spent.

Head-to-Head Comparison of Wireless Headphones

This section provides a detailed breakdown of each model's performance, highlighting their distinct strengths and weaknesses as revealed by the criterion scores.

Sony WH-CH720N: The Comfort Champion

The Sony WH-CH720N excels as a comfortable, everyday headphone with reliable performance. Its defining characteristic is its 5/5 score in Comfort, achieved through an extremely lightweight design and well-padded earcups that avoid excessive clamping pressure. This makes it a top candidate for users who wear headphones for hours on end.

Its 5/5 Battery Life score is equally impressive, offering 35 hours of playback even with ANC activated. However, the product makes noticeable trade-offs to achieve its lightweight and affordable design. The Sound Quality score of 3/5 reflects a somewhat bass-light and compressed profile that lacks the richness of more expensive models. Furthermore, the Build Quality score of 2/5 indicates a plasticky construction that can feel creaky and less durable. Its noise cancellation is effective for constant hums (4/5) but not for irregular sounds. Overall, it represents strong value (4/5) for those prioritizing comfort and battery life above all.

Best For: Users who need all-day comfort for work or travel, and value the Sony ecosystem and reliable ANC.
Compromises: Average sound quality and a less premium build feel.

Anker Soundcore Space Q45: The Feature-Packed All-Rounder (Winner)

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 wins by delivering exceptional performance where it counts most, without a single major weakness. It stands out immediately with a class-leading 5/5 Battery Life score, boasting over 50 hours with ANC on—a figure that outperforms many competitors and eliminates daily charging worries.

It complements this with a strong 4/5 score in Sound Quality, providing well-balanced, detailed audio that works across music, podcasts, and movies. Its hybrid ANC is competent for the price (3/5), and comfort is very good thanks to plush memory foam (4/5). While its build is functional plastic (3/5), it’s the product's overall 5/5 Value for Money that seals its victory. The Space Q45 packs features and battery endurance typically found in headphones costing $100 more, making it the best overall package for the budget-conscious buyer who refuses to compromise on core features.

Best For: The value-seeking user who wants premium-tier battery life and balanced performance across all criteria.
Compromises: ANC is good but not best-in-class; design can feel slightly bulky.

JBL Tune 770NC: The Bass-Powered Entertainer

The JBL Tune 770NC targets listeners who want powerful, energetic sound and marathon battery life on a strict budget. Its sound signature is distinctly bass-heavy, which is enjoyable for hip-hop, EDM, and action movies but results in a 3/5 Sound Quality score due to muddiness at high volumes and overshadowed mids/treble.

It shines in endurance, with a 4/5 Battery Life score reflecting its impressive 70-hour claim (without ANC). Comfort is above average (4/5) with a foldable design, though it can feel bulky. The trade-offs are evident in Noise Cancellation (3/5), which is merely adequate, and Build Quality (2/5), where the plastic feels least premium of the group. Its strong 4/5 Value for Money score comes from delivering ANC and epic battery life at a very accessible $99 price point.

Best For: Budget-focused listeners who prioritize powerful bass and extremely long battery life for entertainment.
Compromises: Less accurate sound, average ANC and build quality.

Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT: The Studio-Accurate Purist

The Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT takes a fundamentally different approach, forgoing modern ANC to focus on sound accuracy and reliability. It earns a 4/5 in Sound Quality by providing a balanced, clear frequency response ideal for critical listening, mixing, and appreciating musical detail—a hallmark of the brand's studio heritage.

It also boasts a very long 60-hour battery life (4/5) and can be used wired even with a dead battery. However, the lack of active noise cancellation results in a 2/5 score in that category, a significant disadvantage in noisy environments. Its comfort (3/5) and build (3/5) are utilitarian and focused on durability over luxury. It represents strong value (4/5) for a specific user: someone who prioritizes sound fidelity above all else and primarily listens in quiet settings.

Best For: Audiophiles on a budget, aspiring producers, and listeners in quiet environments who value accurate sound over features.
Compromises: No ANC at all; comfort and style are utilitarian.

Verdict & Recommendations

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 is the recommended choice for most buyers seeking wireless headphones under $130. It wins by avoiding major weaknesses and excelling in critical areas: its battery life is best-in-class, its sound quality is balanced and detailed, and it offers a complete feature set including customizable ANC. It represents the pinnacle of value in this comparison, delivering a high-performance experience that challenges more expensive brands.

However, the "best" headphone is ultimately the one that best fits your personal needs:

  • Choose the Sony WH-CH720N if your top priority is all-day, forget-they’re-there comfort, and you value Sony's reliable ANC and user experience.
  • Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if you are on a tight budget, love a powerful bass response for movies and music, and need the absolute longest possible battery life.
  • Choose the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT if accurate, uncolored sound is your non-negotiable requirement for critical listening or content creation, and you do not need active noise cancellation.

All four models offer compelling reasons for purchase. By aligning the strengths of each with your personal weighting of sound, comfort, features, and budget, you can make a confident and satisfying decision.
