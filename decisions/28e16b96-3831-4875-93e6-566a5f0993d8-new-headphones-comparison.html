New Headphones Comparison

Published on January 24, 2026

Compare wireless headphones and earbuds

Choosing the right wireless headphones involves balancing several competing priorities: sound quality, noise cancellation, comfort, battery life, and price. This comparison evaluates four popular models in the budget to mid-range segment, each offering a distinct blend of features and performance. The analysis reveals that while all contenders have merit, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 emerges as the overall champion. It achieves this by delivering exceptional battery life, strong sound quality, and capable noise cancellation at a highly competitive price point. For buyers seeking the best combination of features without a significant investment, the Space Q45 represents a compelling package. The Sony WH-CH720N follows closely, distinguished by its class-leading comfort, while the JBL Tune 770NC and Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT cater to specific listener preferences for powerful bass and accurate sound, respectively.

Methodology

This comparison uses a weighted scoring system to objectively evaluate each headphone across six key criteria. Each criterion is assigned a weight reflecting its relative importance to the overall user experience for wireless audio. Products receive a score from 1 to 5 for each category, which is then multiplied by the criterion's weight. The final score is the sum of these weighted results, providing a clear, quantified ranking that highlights each model's strengths and trade-offs.

Understanding the Evaluation Criteria

Selecting headphones is a personal decision, but certain universal factors determine satisfaction. The criteria below form the basis of this comparison.

  • Sound Quality (Weight: 2.0): This is the core function of any headphone. It assesses audio clarity, depth of bass, crispness of treble, and overall balance. A higher score indicates a more accurate, detailed, and enjoyable listening experience across various music genres.
  • Noise Cancellation (Weight: 1.8): Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) uses microphones and inverse sound waves to reduce ambient noise. Effectiveness varies, with premium systems canceling a wider range of frequencies (from airplane rumble to office chatter). This criterion evaluates how well each model creates a quiet personal listening space.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.5): Headphones are often worn for hours. Comfort encompasses the weight, clamping force, padding material on the headband and ear cups, and overall fit. A comfortable design prevents fatigue and allows for extended use.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.3): Wireless freedom depends on battery endurance. This score reflects the hours of playback on a single charge, a critical factor for travelers, commuters, and those who wish to avoid daily charging.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.2): This measures the durability of materials and construction. It includes the feel of plastics, metals, and fabrics, the robustness of hinges and sliders, and the product's likelihood of withstanding regular use.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): Perhaps the most holistic criterion, value assesses the total package of features, performance, and build relative to the price. A high-value product delivers performance that meets or exceeds expectations for its cost.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Sony WH-CH720N: The Comfort Champion

The Sony WH-CH720N prioritizes lightweight comfort and reliable performance. It scores a perfect 5/5 in both Comfort and Battery Life, making it an ideal choice for long listening sessions and travel. The noise cancellation is effective for its price, earning a 4/5, though it may struggle with sudden, sharp noises.

However, compromises are made to achieve its featherweight design and attractive price. The Sound Quality is rated 3/5, described as clear but somewhat bass-light and lacking richness. The Build Quality receives the lowest score in this comparison at 2/5, with a plastic construction that can feel insubstantial. For users who prioritize all-day wearability and long battery life above premium materials or audiophile-grade sound, the WH-CH720N is a top contender.

Anker Soundcore Space Q45: The Overall Value Leader

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 wins with a balanced, high-performance feature set. It excels in two key areas: a perfect 5/5 in Battery Life, boasting over 50 hours with ANC on, and a top score of 5/5 in Value for Money. Its Sound Quality is also strong at 4/5, offering clear, well-balanced audio.

Its Noise Cancellation is competent (3/5) but not class-leading, and its Build Quality (3/5) is adequate though not premium. Some users may also find its design slightly bulky. The Space Q45’s triumph is its ability to deliver premium-tier features like LDAC support and customizable ANC at a mid-range price, offering an exceptional blend of performance and cost that resonates with most buyers.

JBL Tune 770NC: The Bass-Powered Budget Option

The JBL Tune 770NC targets listeners who want powerful sound and maximum battery life on a strict budget. Its standout feature is an incredible 70-hour battery life with ANC off, scoring 4/5 in this category. It also provides good comfort (4/5) and strong value (4/5) at its sub-$100 price.

These benefits come with clear trade-offs. The Sound Quality (3/5) is characterized by a dominant, sometimes overwhelming bass that can muddy other frequencies. The Noise Cancellation (3/5) is basic, and the Build Quality (2/5) feels plasticky and less durable. This model is best for casual listeners who crave a bass-heavy signature and rarely want to charge their headphones.

Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT: The Studio Sound Purist

The Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT takes a different approach, forgoing popular features to focus on audio accuracy and simplicity. It achieves a high score of 4/5 in Sound Quality, delivering the balanced, detailed sound typical of studio headphones. It also offers an excellent 60-hour battery life (4/5).

The most significant limitation is the complete lack of Active Noise Cancellation, resulting in a 2/5 score. It relies solely on passive isolation. Its comfort (3/5) and build (3/5) are functional but unremarkable. This model is the clear choice for aspiring audio engineers, podcasters, or listeners who prioritize uncolored, accurate sound reproduction above all else and do not require ANC.

Verdict and Recommendations

Winner: Anker Soundcore Space Q45
The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 is the recommended choice for most buyers seeking wireless over-ear headphones. It claims victory by delivering no major weaknesses and exceptional strengths in the most impactful areas: marathon battery life and outstanding value for money. Its sound quality surpasses most in its class, and its customizable ANC via app adds useful flexibility. For under $130, it provides a feature set and performance that challenge much more expensive models, making it the most versatile and sensible purchase.

When to Choose an Alternative:

  • Choose the Sony WH-CH720N if: Extreme comfort for all-day wear is your absolute top priority. Its exceptionally lightweight design is unmatched in this group. It’s also a great pick if strong call quality and multi-point Bluetooth connectivity are daily necessities.
  • Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if: You are on a tight budget and your primary goal is to feel powerful, deep bass in your music, movies, and games. Its phenomenal battery life is a major bonus for those who frequently forget to charge.
  • Choose the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT if: Accurate, balanced sound for critical listening, podcast editing, or studio monitoring is essential, and you have no need for active noise cancellation. It offers great sound and battery life at a very accessible price for an entry-level studio tool.
