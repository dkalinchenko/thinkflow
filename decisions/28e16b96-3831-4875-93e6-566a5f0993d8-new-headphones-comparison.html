Published on January 24, 2026

Compare wireless headphones and earbuds

Choosing the right pair of wireless headphones is a balance of priorities. For some, immersive sound and noise cancellation are non-negotiable. For others, marathon battery life or all-day comfort takes precedence. With a crowded market ranging from budget-friendly to premium, identifying the best value and performance for your specific needs is the key challenge.

This comparison focuses on four prominent over-ear wireless headphones in the affordable to mid-range segment: the Sony WH-CH720N, Anker Soundcore Space Q45, JBL Tune 770NC, and Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT. Each model stakes its claim with distinct strengths, from Sony’s featherweight comfort to JBL’s thunderous bass and Audio-Technica’s studio heritage. After a detailed evaluation across six weighted criteria, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 emerges as the overall winner, offering the most compelling blend of features, battery life, and sound quality for its price.

The evaluation was conducted using a weighted scoring system to reflect the varying importance of different features to a typical buyer. Each headphone was rated from 1 to 5 on six key criteria: Sound Quality (weight: 2), Noise Cancellation (weight: 1.8), Comfort (weight: 1.5), Battery Life (weight: 1.3), Build Quality (weight: 1.2), and Value for Money (weight: 1.5). These weighted scores were then tallied to determine the final ranking.

Criteria Deep-Dive: What to Look For

Understanding the criteria is essential for aligning a purchase with personal needs.

  • Sound Quality (Weight: 2): The most important factor, this assesses audio clarity, frequency balance, and detail. A high score indicates a well-tuned, enjoyable sound profile suitable for a wide range of music and media.
  • Noise Cancellation (Weight: 1.8): Critical for commuters, travelers, and office workers, this measures the effectiveness of Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) in reducing ambient noise, from low-frequency rumbles to chatter.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.5): For long listening sessions, factors like weight, clamp force, earcup padding, and material breathability are paramount. A comfortable headphone can be worn for hours without fatigue.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.3): This scores the hours of playback on a single charge. Longer battery life enhances convenience and reliability, especially for travel.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.2): This evaluates the durability and feel of materials used. A higher score suggests a product that will withstand daily use and feel more premium.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): A holistic measure of what you get for the price. The best value doesn’t always mean the cheapest; it’s the product that delivers the most desired features and performance relative to its cost.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Sony WH-CH720N: The Comfort Champion

Sony’s WH-CH720N focuses on delivering core features—noise cancellation and battery life—in an exceptionally lightweight package.

Strengths:

  • Unmatched Comfort: With a score of 5/5, its featherweight design and well-padded earcups make it ideal for all-day wear without fatigue.
  • Excellent Endurance: A 5/5 battery life score reflects its class-leading 35 hours of playback with ANC active.
  • Competent ANC: Scoring 4/5, its noise cancellation effectively tackles constant low-frequency noise, a significant benefit at this price.

Weaknesses:

  • Compromised Build: To achieve its low weight, Sony used plastics that score a 2/5 for build quality, resulting in a less premium feel and some creakiness.
  • Bass-Light Sound: Its sound quality score of 3/5 notes a clear but somewhat thin default profile that lacks richness and impactful bass.

Bottom Line: The Sony WH-CH720N is the top recommendation for users who prioritize comfort above all else and want reliable ANC and battery life in a no-fuss package, accepting trade-offs in build and sonic weight.

Anker Soundcore Space Q45: The Balanced Performer

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 wins by being excellent, not necessarily the best, in every category, culminating in the highest total score.

Strengths:

  • Outstanding Value & Battery: It earns a perfect 5/5 in both Value for Money and Battery Life, offering over 50 hours of ANC-on playback and features typically found in more expensive models.
  • Well-Rounded Audio: With a 4/5 sound quality score, it delivers clear, balanced audio with good detail and a customizable EQ via its companion app.
  • Strong Feature Set: Includes high-resolution LDAC codec support and effective hybrid ANC (scoring 3/5), which is competent for the price.

Weaknesses:

  • Bulky Design: Some users may find the fit a bit bulky, and the carrying case is notably large.
  • Average Call Quality: The microphone performance is just adequate for voice calls.

Bottom Line: The Space Q45 doesn’t have a single glaring weakness. It provides the most consistent, high-level performance across all criteria, making it the best all-rounder and overall winner for most buyers.

JBL Tune 770NC: The Bass Powerhouse

JBL’s Tune 770NC caters to a specific listener: one who wants powerful, energetic sound and marathon battery life on a tight budget.

Strengths:

  • High-Energy Sound: It delivers JBL’s signature powerful bass (though this contributes to its 3/5 sound quality score, as it can overwhelm other frequencies).
  • Exceptional Battery: A key selling point, it offers up to 70 hours without ANC, scoring 4/5 for battery life.
  • Budget-Friendly Price: At $99, it delivers decent ANC and features, scoring 4/5 for value.

Weaknesses:

  • Unbalanced Audio: The bass-heavy signature lacks the clarity and balance of its competitors, which can make music sound muddy.
  • Subpar Build Quality: Scoring 2/5, its plasticky construction raises concerns about long-term durability.

Bottom Line: The JBL Tune 770NC is the clear choice for budget-conscious bass lovers who prioritize energetic sound and the longest possible battery life over nuanced audio and premium construction.

Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT: The Purist's Choice

The Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT takes a different approach, focusing on sound accuracy and reliability while omitting a key modern feature.

Strengths:

  • Accurate, Balanced Sound: True to its studio heritage, it scores 4/5 for sound quality, offering excellent clarity and a balanced frequency response ideal for critical listening.
  • Superb Battery Life: With 60 hours of playback, it scores 4/5, ensuring long intervals between charges.
  • Wired Versatility: It can be used passively with the included cable even when the battery is dead.

Weaknesses:

  • No Active Noise Cancellation: The most significant drawback, scoring 2/5. It relies solely on passive isolation, which is ineffective in noisy environments.
  • Utilitarian Design: The studio-focused design is less stylish and may not be as comfortable for all-day casual wear as other models (3/5 comfort score).

Bottom Line: The ATH-M20xBT is the ideal pick for purists, students, or content creators who prioritize accurate, uncolored sound and battery life in quiet environments and can live without ANC.

Verdict & Final Recommendations

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 stands as the champion in this comparison for a clear reason: it masters the art of balance. It doesn’t sacrifice battery life for sound, or comfort for features. By delivering excellent endurance, very good audio quality, competent noise cancellation, and high comfort at a competitive price, it represents the safest and most rewarding choice for the average user. Its perfect score in Value for Money underscores its position as the product that gives you the most for what you spend.

However, the "best" headphone is ultimately the one that best fits your personal profile:

  • Choose the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 if: You want the best overall package—great battery, good sound, decent ANC, and top value—without any major compromises.
  • Choose the Sony WH-CH720N if: All-day comfort is your absolute top priority, and you want lightweight, reliable ANC from a trusted brand.
  • Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if: You crave powerful, bass-driven sound and need the absolute longest battery life possible on a strict budget.
  • Choose the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT if: You value accurate, balanced sound for critical listening or content creation in quiet settings and have no need for ANC.
