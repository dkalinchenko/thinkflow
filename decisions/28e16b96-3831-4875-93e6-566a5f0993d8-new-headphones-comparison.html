New Headphones Comparison

Published on January 24, 2026

Compare wireless headphones and earbuds

Choosing the right wireless headphones involves balancing competing priorities like sound quality, noise cancellation, comfort, and price. In the competitive market of mid-range audio gear, four models stand out: the Sony WH-CH720N, Anker Soundcore Space Q45, JBL Tune 770NC, and Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT. Each offers a distinct blend of features catering to different listener profiles, from the frequent traveler to the budget-conscious audiophile. After a detailed evaluation against weighted criteria, the Anker Soundcore Space Q45 emerges as the overall champion, securing the top spot due to its exceptional balance of battery life, sound performance, and value. However, the close scores reveal that the "best" choice heavily depends on individual needs, with Sony leading in comfort and Audio-Technica excelling in pure sound accuracy for the price.

Methodology

This comparison employs a weighted scoring system to objectively assess each headphone model. Six key criteria—Sound Quality, Noise Cancellation, Comfort, Battery Life, Build Quality, and Value for Money—were evaluated. Each criterion was assigned a weight reflecting its relative importance to the average buyer in this category (e.g., Sound Quality has a higher weight than Build Quality). Every product received a score from 1 to 5 for each criterion, which was then multiplied by the criterion's weight. The final scores and rankings are the sum of these weighted results, ensuring the analysis reflects both performance and priority.

Understanding the Evaluation Criteria

Selecting headphones requires knowing what aspects impact the listening experience most. Here’s why each criterion in this comparison matters:

  • Sound Quality (Weight: 2): The primary function of any headphone is audio reproduction. This criterion assesses clarity, bass response, treble detail, and overall balance. A high score indicates a pleasing, accurate, or engaging sound signature suitable for a wide range of music and media.
  • Noise Cancellation (Weight: 1.8): Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) uses microphones and inverse sound waves to reduce ambient noise. Its effectiveness is crucial for concentration in noisy offices, peaceful travel, or immersive listening without cranking up the volume.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.5): Headphones are worn for extended periods. This evaluates fit, weight, clamping force, and padding quality. Poor comfort can render even the best-sounding headphones unusable for long sessions.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.3): Wireless freedom is dependent on battery endurance. This score reflects hours of playback on a single charge, impacting how often users need to recharge and their viability for long trips or daily use.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.2): This assesses the durability of materials and construction. A higher score suggests a product that will withstand regular use and feel more premium, though it often correlates with a higher price.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): Perhaps the most holistic criterion, this measures the overall package offered relative to the price. It balances performance, features, and build to determine which product delivers the most for the user's investment.

Head-to-Head Headphone Analysis

Sony WH-CH720N: The Featherweight Champion

The Sony WH-CH720N prioritizes comfort and efficiency, making a compelling case for users who wear headphones all day.

  • Strengths: Its standout feature is exceptional comfort, earning a perfect 5/5 score. The extremely lightweight design and well-padded ear cups minimize fatigue during marathon sessions. This is paired with best-in-class battery management, offering 35 hours of playback with ANC activated. The noise cancellation is effective for its price, proficiently handling constant low-frequency noises.
  • Weaknesses: Trade-offs are evident in build and sound refinement. The plastic construction feels less premium and can creak, resulting in a below-average 2/5 Build Quality score. The default sound profile is clear but somewhat bass-light and lacks the detail of more audio-focused competitors, reflected in its mid-tier 3/5 Sound Quality score.
  • Best For: Students, remote workers, or commuters who prioritize all-day comfort and reliable battery life over absolute sonic perfection or a luxury feel.

Anker Soundcore Space Q45: The Balanced Performer

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 wins the overall comparison by delivering no single weak score and excelling in key areas, offering a remarkably complete package.

  • Strengths: It achieves top marks in Battery Life (5/5) and Value for Money (5/5), boasting an industry-leading 50+ hours with ANC on. Its 4/5 Sound Quality score indicates a well-balanced, detailed audio profile that satisfies most listeners. Features like LDAC support and customizable ANC via an app add polish usually found in more expensive models.
  • Weaknesses: The most notable compromise is in noise cancellation. While good, its 3/5 score shows it can't match the absolute silence provided by premium brands. Some users may also find the design slightly bulky, and the microphone quality is noted as average.
  • Best For: The value-seeking user who wants a feature-rich, no-compromise daily driver with marathon battery life, great sound, and competent ANC without paying a premium brand tax.

JBL Tune 770NC: The Bass-Powered Bargain

The JBL Tune 770NC leans into a fun, energetic sound signature and incredible battery efficiency, targeting listeners who want impactful audio at a low cost.

  • Strengths: It delivers JBL's signature powerful bass response, which many casual listeners enjoy, and an astounding 70-hour battery life with ANC off. Its foldable design and included AUX cable enhance practicality. For its sub-$100 price, it packs in ANC and decent comfort (4/5), offering strong value.
  • Weaknesses: The bass-heavy sound can muddy vocals and intricate details, limiting its appeal for critical listening (Sound Quality: 3/5). The ANC is basic, and the build quality (2/5) feels plasticky and less durable than competitors.
  • Best For: Budget-conscious buyers and bass lovers who want wireless convenience, extremely long battery life, and engaging (though not precise) sound for casual listening and entertainment.

Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT: The Purist's Choice

The Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT takes a different approach, forgoing trendy features like ANC to focus on accurate sound reproduction and reliable functionality.

  • Strengths: It excels in sound accuracy, earning a 4/5 for Sound Quality with a balanced, clear frequency response ideal for critical listening, mixing, and appreciating music details. Its 60-hour battery life is also a major asset. The ability to use them wired even with a dead battery is a unique and practical advantage.
  • Weaknesses: The lack of active noise cancellation is its most significant limitation, scoring a 2/5 in that category. The utilitarian studio design may lack aesthetic appeal for some, and the comfort, while decent, is not as plush as more consumer-focused models.
  • Best For: Aspiring audio engineers, purists, and listeners in quiet environments who prioritize uncolored, accurate sound and long battery life above all else, and who do not require ANC.

Verdict and Recommendations

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 is the recommended choice for most buyers seeking wireless headphones. It wins not by having a single knockout feature, but by having no major flaws and leading its category in crucial areas like battery life and overall value. It successfully bridges the gap between budget and premium tiers, offering a cohesive experience that satisfies across all evaluated criteria.

However, the close competition underscores that the optimal choice is personal:

  • Choose the Sony WH-CH720N if all-day comfort is your non-negotiable priority. Its lightweight design is unmatched, making it ideal for work, study, or long commutes where physical fatigue is a concern.
  • Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if you are on a strict budget and crave powerful, bass-forward sound for movies, hip-hop, and EDM. Its incredible battery life offers maximum convenience for the price.
  • Choose the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT if you are a discerning listener, podcaster, or content creator who values sound accuracy and reliability over noise-cancelling features. It delivers studio-grade audio on a budget.

Ultimately, aligning the strengths of each model with your personal listening habits, environment, and budget will lead to the most satisfying purchase.
