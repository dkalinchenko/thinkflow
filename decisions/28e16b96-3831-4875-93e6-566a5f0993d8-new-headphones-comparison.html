Published on January 24, 2026

Compare wireless headphones and earbuds

Choosing the right wireless headphones involves balancing numerous factors, from immersive sound and effective noise cancellation to long-lasting comfort and battery life. With prices ranging from budget-friendly to premium, identifying the model that delivers the best combination of features for your needs is key. This comparison evaluates four popular models—the Sony WH-CH720N, Anker Soundcore Space Q45, JBL Tune 770NC, and Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT—across six critical performance areas to determine a clear winner.

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 emerges as the top overall pick, achieving the highest score by excelling in core areas like battery life and value while delivering strong, balanced performance across the board. However, the ideal choice ultimately depends on individual priorities: the Sony WH-CH720N is unparalleled in comfort, the JBL Tune 770NC offers massive battery life on a tight budget, and the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT provides superior sound accuracy for purists. This analysis breaks down each model’s strengths and weaknesses to guide the purchasing decision.

Methodology

This evaluation employs a weighted scoring system to objectively compare each pair of headphones. Six key criteria—Sound Quality, Noise Cancellation, Comfort, Battery Life, Build Quality, and Value for Money—were assigned weights based on their relative importance to the average user. Each headphone was then scored on a scale of 1 to 5 for every criterion. The final score is a weighted sum of these individual ratings, ensuring that higher-priority factors have a greater impact on the overall result and the final ranking.

Evaluation Criteria Deep-Dive

Understanding the criteria is essential for interpreting the scores and aligning them with personal needs.

  • Sound Quality (Weight: 2.0): This is the most heavily weighted factor, as it defines the core listening experience. It assesses audio clarity, bass depth, treble balance, and overall detail. A higher score indicates a more balanced, rich, and accurate sound suitable for a wide range of music genres.
  • Noise Cancellation (Weight: 1.8): Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) technology uses microphones to counteract ambient sound. This criterion evaluates how effectively a headphone can reduce constant low-frequency noise (like airplane engines) and more variable sounds (like conversations), which is crucial for travel, commuting, and focused work.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.5): Headphones are often worn for hours. This score considers fit, weight, clamping force, and the quality of padding on the headband and ear cups. A comfortable design prevents fatigue and makes extended listening sessions enjoyable.
  • Battery Life (Weight: 1.3): Measured in hours of playback, this impacts daily convenience and charging frequency. Models with longer battery life are better suited for travel, long workdays, or users who prefer not to charge their devices daily.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.2): This reflects the durability of materials (plastic, metal, leatherette) and construction, including hinges and joints. A higher build quality score suggests a product that will better withstand regular use and feel more premium.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): Perhaps the most holistic criterion, it judges whether the headphone’s overall package of features, performance, and build justifies its price point. A high-value product delivers performance that meets or exceeds expectations for its cost.

Head-to-Head Comparison: Detailed Analysis

Sony WH-CH720N: The Comfort and Connectivity Champion

The Sony WH-CH720N excels by prioritizing lightweight comfort and seamless usability, making it an excellent choice for all-day wear.

  • Strengths: With a perfect 5/5 score in Comfort, these headphones are exceptionally lightweight with ample padding, minimizing fatigue during long sessions. They also achieve a 5/5 in Battery Life, offering a robust 35 hours of playback with ANC active. The effective noise cancellation (scored 4/5) handles consistent low-frequency noise well, and features like multi-point Bluetooth connectivity add significant practical value.
  • Weaknesses: The primary compromises are in Build Quality (2/5) and Sound Quality (3/5). The plastic construction can feel creaky and less durable. The default sound profile is clear but somewhat bass-light and lacks the richness of more audio-focused models.
  • Best For: Users who prioritize all-day comfort, long battery life, and reliable ANC for commuting or office use, and who value seamless switching between devices.

Anker Soundcore Space Q45: The Balanced All-Rounder (Winner)

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 wins by delivering no major weaknesses and exceptional performance in key areas, particularly battery life and value.

  • Strengths: This model stands out with a class-leading Battery Life score of 5/5, offering over 50 hours with ANC on. It also earns top marks for Value for Money (5/5), packing features typically found in more expensive headphones. Sound Quality is strong at 4/5, providing clear, well-balanced audio that works for most genres. Comfort is also high at 4/5, thanks to plush memory foam ear cups.
  • Weaknesses: The main trade-offs are moderate. The Noise Cancellation (3/5) is effective for its price but doesn’t match premium competitors, especially with higher-frequency sounds. The Build Quality (3/5) is adequate but uses plastics that can feel less premium and may produce some creaking.
  • Best For: The buyer seeking the best overall package—outstanding battery, good sound, solid ANC, and high comfort—at a mid-range price point. It’s the most well-rounded choice.

JBL Tune 770NC: The Bass-Powered Budget Option

The JBL Tune 770NC focuses on delivering powerful, bass-forward sound and impressive battery life at a very accessible price.

  • Strengths: This model offers remarkable value, highlighted by a 4/5 score in Value for Money and a 4/5 in Battery Life, with some of the longest endurance in the comparison. Its Comfort score of 4/5 comes from a decently padded, foldable design. The sound profile, while specific, delivers powerful, deep bass favored in many pop and electronic genres.
  • Weaknesses: The bass-heavy sound comes at the cost of overall Sound Quality balance (3/5), which can sound muddy and overwhelm mids and treble. The Noise Cancellation is only average (3/5), and the Build Quality is a low point at 2/5, with a plasticky feel that raises durability concerns.
  • Best For: Budget-conscious listeners who prioritize long battery life and enjoy a powerful, bass-emphasized sound signature for casual listening.

Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT: The Sound Purist's Choice

The Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT takes a different approach, forgoing popular features like ANC to focus on accurate sound reproduction and reliability.

  • Strengths: As expected from Audio-Technica’s studio heritage, it scores a 4/5 in Sound Quality for its excellent clarity and balanced frequency response, making it ideal for critical listening. It also boasts a very long 60-hour Battery Life (4/5). The ability to function as wired headphones even with a dead battery is a significant practical advantage.
  • Weaknesses: The most notable omission is active noise cancellation, resulting in a low 2/5 score in that category. It relies solely on passive isolation, which is less effective in noisy environments. The utilitarian design scores moderately for Comfort (3/5) and Build Quality (3/5), lacking the plushness or premium materials of other models.
  • Best For: Listeners, students, or hobbyists who prioritize accurate, balanced audio quality for music production, podcast editing, or pure listening enjoyment in quiet to moderately quiet settings, and who do not require ANC.

Verdict & Recommendations

The Anker Soundcore Space Q45 is the recommended choice for most buyers, winning with a balanced score of 37.00 points. It successfully avoids any major pitfalls while excelling in critical areas: its battery life is exceptional, its sound quality is well-rounded, and it delivers these features at a price that represents outstanding value. For the user who wants a single pair of headphones that reliably performs well across music, travel, and work without breaking the bank, the Space Q45 is the optimal selection.

However, specific needs may make an alternative the better choice:

  • Choose the Sony WH-CH720N if all-day, marathon comfort is your top priority. Its lightweight design is unmatched, and it couples this with very good ANC and battery life, making it perfect for long-haul travelers, office workers, or students with back-to-back classes.
  • Choose the JBL Tune 770NC if you are on a strict budget and love powerful bass. It delivers fun, energetic sound and incredible battery longevity for its low price, ideal for casual listeners who want an engaging, low-end focused experience without spending much.
  • Choose the Audio-Technica ATH-M20xBT if accurate, studio-quality sound is your foremost concern and you can live without ANC. It’s the best tool for critical listening, content creation, or anyone who prefers a balanced, uncolored audio profile over feature-packed convenience.

Ultimately, the "best" headphones are the ones that align most closely with your personal listening habits, comfort requirements, and budget. Use this detailed comparison to weigh the trade-offs and select the model that turns your music, podcasts, and calls into a superior auditory experience.
