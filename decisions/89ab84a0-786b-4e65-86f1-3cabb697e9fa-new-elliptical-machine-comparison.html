Compare ellipticals for low-impact cardio

Choosing the right elliptical machine involves balancing multiple factors: stride comfort, resistance smoothness, durability, and overall value. For users seeking effective, low-impact cardio, the wrong choice can lead to discomfort, underuse, and wasted investment. After a detailed evaluation of four leading models, the Sole Fitness E35 Elliptical emerges as the top recommendation for most users. It delivers an exceptional combination of commercial-grade build quality, a highly adjustable stride, and supremely smooth, joint-friendly motion, justifying its premium price with long-term durability and performance. The NordicTrack FS10i offers revolutionary versatility, while the ProForm Carbon EL presents a compelling budget-friendly option, but the Sole E35 provides the most balanced and reliable package for dedicated home cardio training.

The evaluation was conducted using a weighted scoring system across six key criteria: Stride Length, Resistance, Build Quality, Features, Joint Impact, and Value for Money. Each criterion was assigned a weight reflecting its relative importance for low-impact cardio training, with scores out of 5 given to each elliptical based on detailed performance assessments. The total weighted score determined the final ranking.

Understanding the Evaluation Criteria for Ellipticals

Selecting an elliptical isn't just about price or brand; specific technical and ergonomic factors directly impact workout quality, comfort, and longevity. These criteria form the foundation of a smart purchase decision.

  • Stride Length (Weight: 2): This is arguably the most critical factor for comfort and effectiveness. A stride that is too short creates a cramped, unnatural feeling, while one that is too long can cause over-extension. Adjustable or variable stride lengths accommodate different user heights and leg lengths, promoting a natural gait that mimics running or walking without impact. A proper stride ensures efficient muscle engagement and makes longer workouts sustainable.
  • Resistance (Weight: 1.8): The quality and smoothness of the resistance system define the workout experience. Magnetic resistance is generally quieter and smoother than friction-based systems. The number of levels provides granular control over intensity, and a heavy flywheel contributes to a consistent, fluid pedaling motion. A jerky or noisy resistance mechanism can ruin immersion and motivation.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.7): This encompasses the machine's stability, weight capacity, and the durability of its components. A sturdy steel frame with a high user weight capacity feels secure during vigorous workouts and is built to last for years. Poor build quality often manifests as wobbling, creaking, and premature failure of parts, leading to costly repairs or early replacement.
  • Features (Weight: 1.5): This category includes workout programs, power incline/decline, console displays, and connectivity (Bluetooth, app integration, touchscreens). Features enhance workout variety and engagement. Interactive coaching subscriptions like iFit or JRNY can provide guided training but often add an ongoing cost.
  • Joint Impact (Weight: 1.6): The primary reason many choose an elliptical is for low-impact exercise. A well-designed elliptical path minimizes stress on the knees, hips, and back. Smooth motion without jarring transitions is essential for users with arthritis, previous injuries, or those seeking active recovery. The machine should provide cardiovascular benefits without joint strain.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): Value assesses the balance between upfront cost and the long-term return in terms of quality, features, and durability. The cheapest option may have hidden costs in limited lifespan, while the most expensive may offer features not all users need. True value aligns price with the user's specific requirements and expected usage.

Head-to-Head Elliptical Analysis

Sole Fitness E35 Elliptical: The Durability Champion

The Sole Fitness E35 is built like commercial gym equipment, designed for serious, long-term home use. Its standout feature is the adjustable 18- to 24-inch stride, which earned a perfect 5/5 score, allowing users of virtually any height to find a perfectly natural and comfortable motion. This adjustability, combined with a heavy 30-pound flywheel, contributes to its also-perfect 5/5 score in Joint Impact, providing one of the smoothest and most joint-protective motions available.

Its Build Quality is exceptional (5/5), featuring heavy-duty steel construction and a 400 lb weight capacity that ensures remarkable stability and durability. Where it makes trade-offs is in Features (3/5); while it offers solid workout programs and metrics, it lacks modern smart connectivity and its console interface can feel dated. Its Value for Money score is a modest 2/5, reflecting its high initial investment. However, for users who prioritize a rock-solid machine, a flawless stride, and joint health over interactive gadgets, this cost translates into decades of reliable service.

  • Best For: Tall users, heavier individuals, families with multiple users, and anyone seeking a "buy-it-for-life" machine with unparalleled smoothness and stability.
  • Compromise: Higher upfront cost and fewer interactive tech features.

NordicTrack FS10i Freestride Trainer: The Versatility Innovator

The NordicTrack FS10i is not a traditional elliptical; it's a Freestride Trainer with revolutionary variable stride technology. Its stride can adjust from 0 to 32 inches automatically during iFit workouts, earning a 5/5 in Stride Length. This allows it to uniquely simulate walking, jogging, running, stair climbing, and elliptical gliding in one machine, offering unmatched workout variety.

It scores a top-tier 5/5 in Features, boasting a large touchscreen, automatic incline/decline, and full iFit integration for global workout simulations and live coaching. Its motion is smooth and Joint Impact is high (4/5), though the extreme range of motion may feel less natural to some. The primary drawbacks are cost and complexity. With a high purchase price and a near-mandatory iFit subscription, its Value for Money score is low (2/5). Some users also note its Build Quality (3/5), while good, doesn't feel as overbuilt as the Sole E35, potentially indicating more maintenance over time.

  • Best For: Users who get bored easily and crave varied, interactive workouts; those who want to simulate outdoor running indoors without impact.
  • Compromise: Very high total cost of ownership (machine + subscription) and a more complex design.

ProForm Carbon EL: The Smart Value Pick

The ProForm Carbon EL is an attractive option for budget-conscious buyers who still want modern smart features. Its strongest appeal is its Value for Money (3/5) and Features (4/5), offering iFit compatibility, a decent console, and a space-saving design at a significantly lower price point than its competitors. It also provides excellent Joint Impact (5/5) with a smooth, low-impact motion path.

To achieve this price, compromises are made. Its Build Quality is its weakest area (2/5), with a lighter frame and more plastic components that may not withstand intense, daily use over many years. The Stride Length is adjustable but maxes at 20 inches (4/5), which may still feel short for very tall users. The Resistance system is adequate and quiet but not as robust as higher-end models (3/5). It represents a "gateway" smart elliptical—excellent for getting started with connected fitness without a massive investment.

  • Best For: First-time elliptical buyers, users with limited space and budget, and those who want to try iFit-guided workouts without a huge upfront commitment.
  • Compromise: Long-term durability and stability; may feel less substantial during vigorous use.

Bowflex Max Trainer M9: The Hybrid Intensity Machine

It's crucial to understand the Bowflex Max Trainer M9 is a different category of machine. It combines elliptical and stair climber motions into a steep, vertical stride pattern focused on high-intensity interval training (HIIT). This specialization defines its scores. It excels in Features (5/5), with excellent JRNY coaching and programs, and has very good Resistance (4/5) and Build Quality (4/5).

However, for someone seeking a traditional low-impact elliptical, it has significant drawbacks. Its unique motion scores poorly for Joint Impact (2/5), as the climbing action places more strain on the knees and quads. Its fixed Stride Length is vertical, not elliptical, earning a 3/5. Its Value for Money (3/5) is only high if you specifically want this hybrid, high-intensity workout. For pure, smooth, gliding elliptical cardio, it is not the appropriate choice.

  • Best For: Individuals specifically seeking the most time-efficient, high-calorie-burn HIIT workouts in a compact format, and who don't require traditional elliptical motion.
  • Compromise: Not a low-impact elliptical; higher joint strain and a very specialized movement pattern.

Verdict and Final Recommendations

The Sole Fitness E35 Elliptical is the recommended choice for most users seeking a traditional, low-impact cardio machine. It won by delivering top-tier performance in the most critical categories: Stride Length, Build Quality, and Joint Impact. It is the machine least likely to cause discomfort or feel limiting, and it is built to provide smooth, reliable workouts for a decade or more. The investment is substantial, but it buys unparalleled durability and a biomechanically sound motion.

Consider an alternative if:

  • Workout variety and tech integration are your top priorities: The NordicTrack FS10i is in a class of its own for interactive, adaptive training. Be prepared for the ongoing iFit subscription cost.
  • Your budget is under $1,000 and you want smart features: The ProForm Carbon EL is the clear value leader. It’s a great way to start, with the understanding that its longevity may not match premium models.
  • You want maximum calorie burn in minimal time and don’t need a gliding motion: The Bowflex Max Trainer M9 is a superb high-intensity machine, but it should not be purchased expecting a gentle elliptical experience.

When making your final decision, prioritize the factors that align with your primary goal. For pure, joint-friendly, durable elliptical training, the Sole Fitness E35 remains the benchmark.
