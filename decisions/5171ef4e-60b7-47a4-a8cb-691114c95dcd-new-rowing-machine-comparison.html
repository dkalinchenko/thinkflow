Compare rowing machines for full-body workouts

For individuals seeking a comprehensive full-body workout, rowing machines represent one of the most efficient and effective pieces of fitness equipment available. They engage nearly 85% of the body's muscles, providing cardiovascular and strength benefits in one low-impact motion. However, with options ranging from under $250 to over $1,600, selecting the right rower involves navigating significant differences in performance, technology, and build quality. This comparison evaluates four leading models across six critical criteria to identify the best overall value and performance for most users.

After a weighted evaluation, the Concept2 Model D Rowing Machine emerges as the clear winner. While it carries a premium price tag, it excels in the most important categories: Resistance Quality and Build Quality. It is the industry benchmark for performance accuracy and legendary durability, offering a smooth, realistic rowing feel that scales perfectly with user effort. For serious fitness enthusiasts, athletes, or anyone prioritizing a reliable, long-term investment, the Concept2 Model D delivers unmatched value through its proven track record and superior construction. Its main trade-offs are a basic comfort level and lack of a built-in interactive screen, which some connected fitness competitors emphasize.

Methodology

This comparison uses a weighted decision matrix to objectively assess each rowing machine. Six criteria were evaluated based on their importance for a full-body workout machine: Resistance Quality (weight: 2), Build Quality (weight: 1.8), Comfort (weight: 1.6), Monitor & Metrics (weight: 1.5), Storage (weight: 1.4), and Value for Money (weight: 1.5). Each product was scored out of 5 for each criterion. The final weighted score was calculated by multiplying the raw score by the criterion weight and summing the results, providing a balanced view of overall performance relative to user priorities.

Criteria Deep-Dive: What Matters in a Rowing Machine

Choosing a rower involves more than just price. These criteria define the user experience and long-term satisfaction.

  • Resistance Quality (Weight: 2): The heart of the rowing experience. This evaluates the smoothness and realism of the stroke. Air resistance (like Concept2) offers a dynamic, self-regulating drag. Magnetic resistance (Sunny, NordicTrack) provides quiet, consistent resistance. Electromagnetic (Hydrow) combines quiet operation with precise digital control. A superior system feels natural, with smooth engagement and release.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.8): Determines longevity and stability during intense workouts. This includes the durability of the frame, the rigidity of the rail system, and the stated weight capacity. Commercial-grade construction typically uses higher-grade aluminum and steel, minimizing wobble and supporting years of heavy use.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.6): Critical for workout consistency. An uncomfortable seat or poorly positioned footrests can shorten sessions. This assesses the seat cushioning and shape, the adjustability and ergonomics of the footrests, and the design of the handle.
  • Monitor & Metrics (Weight: 1.5): Drives engagement and progress tracking. A basic monitor shows time and strokes. Advanced monitors (PM5, iFIT touchscreens) display detailed metrics like split times, wattage, and heart rate, and often feature connectivity for apps and guided workouts.
  • Storage (Weight: 1.4): A practical necessity for home gyms. This considers whether the machine folds, can be stored vertically, and its overall footprint. Easy storage solutions are highly valuable in space-constrained environments.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): The balance of upfront cost against the durability, features, and overall performance delivered. The highest-priced option isn't always the best value, and the cheapest may incur higher long-term costs if it fails prematurely.

Hydrow Wave Rowing Machine

The Hydrow Wave focuses on an immersive, connected fitness experience with a sleek, modern design. It uses an ultra-quiet electromagnetic resistance system and a large touchscreen to deliver live and on-demand outdoor and studio classes.

  • Performance & Experience: It matches Concept2 with a 5/5 in Resistance Quality due to its exceptionally smooth and silent electromagnetic system. The 22-inch HD touchscreen and immersive content also earn a top score of 5/5 for Monitor & Metrics, creating a highly engaging workout environment.
  • Trade-offs & Cost: The compromises are in Build Quality (3/5), with a frame that is sturdy but not commercial-grade, and Value for Money (2/5). At $1,695 plus a mandatory subscription for full content access, it is a significant investment. Its storage (2/5) is also less convenient than advertised, as it doesn't fold.

Best For: Users who prioritize guided, scenic, or competitive live classes and require extremely quiet operation, and are willing to pay a premium for the connected experience.

Sunny Health & Fitness SF-RW5515 Magnetic Rowing Machine

This model is the budget-friendly entry point, offering basic rowing functionality in a compact, foldable design. It makes rowing accessible but makes clear compromises to achieve its low price.

  • Affordability & Storage: Its greatest strengths are its low $249 price point, which contributes to a 4/5 in Value for Money for beginners, and its 4/5 score in Storage due to its foldable design and small footprint.
  • Performance Limitations: It scores lower in core performance categories: Resistance Quality (3/5) due to only 8 magnetic resistance levels, Build Quality (2/5) with a lower 250 lb weight capacity, and Monitor & Metrics (2/5) with a very basic LCD display lacking connectivity.

Best For: First-time rowers, users with severe space or budget constraints, and those seeking a secondary piece of equipment for light, casual workouts.

NordicTrack RW900 Rowing Machine

The NordicTrack RW900 is a smart rower centered around its large 22-inch HD touchscreen and iFIT interactive training platform. It aims to be an all-in-one connected fitness hub with automated workout adjustments.

  • Interactive Training: Its standout feature is the Monitor & Metrics (5/5), with a large touchscreen that provides access to iFIT's global library of trainer-led workouts, with resistance that can auto-adjust in real-time.
  • Balanced Performance: It offers good Comfort (4/5) and solid Resistance Quality (4/5) with 26 digital levels. However, its Build Quality (3/5) is not as robust as the Concept2, and its Storage (2/5) is cumbersome despite being foldable. The Value for Money (3/5) is heavily tied to the desire for the iFIT ecosystem, as the rowing mechanism itself does not justify the high cost.

Best For: Users already invested in the iFIT ecosystem, those who thrive on guided, interactive workout programs, and who want a large, integrated screen.

Verdict & Final Recommendations

The Concept2 Model D is the definitive winner for most users seeking a rowing machine for serious full-body workouts. It dominates where it matters most: delivering an unbeatable combination of flawless performance dynamics and industrial-strength construction. While you pay more upfront and forgo built-in entertainment, you invest in a tool that will provide accurate, reliable, and challenging workouts for years, if not decades. Its high resale value and status as the competitive standard further cement its top position.

However, the optimal choice depends on individual priorities:

  • Choose the Concept2 Model D if your top priorities are unmatched durability, the most authentic rowing feel, and accurate performance metrics for tracking long-term progress. It is the undisputed workhorse.
  • Choose the Hydrow Wave if an immersive, instructor-led experience with scenic workouts is your main driver, and you value whisper-quiet operation above all else. Be prepared for the highest total cost of ownership.
  • Choose the NordicTrack RW900 if you are deeply committed to interactive, trainer-led programming (iFIT) and want resistance that automatically adjusts within workouts. It is a feature-rich smart rower.
  • Choose the Sunny Health & Fitness SF-RW5515 if you are a beginner testing the waters, have extreme budget limitations, or need the most space-efficient, foldable option for a small apartment.

Ultimately, the best rowing machine aligns with your fitness goals, budget, and space. For a proven, no-frills performer that stands the test of time, the Concept2 Model D remains the benchmark against which all others are measured.
