Compare rowing machines for full-body workouts

Choosing the right rowing machine is a significant investment in long-term health and fitness. This full-body workout tool offers exceptional cardiovascular and muscular benefits, but selecting the wrong model can lead to buyer's remorse, wasted floor space, or underutilized equipment. A rower must balance smooth performance, durable construction, comfortable ergonomics, and practical storage within a budget. The ideal machine should feel motivating to use, withstand years of training, and integrate seamlessly into your lifestyle.

Based on a detailed weighted analysis of the top four contenders, the Concept2 Model D Rowing Machine emerges as the clear winner for most users seeking a serious, durable workout tool. It excelled in the most critical categories—Resistance Quality and Build Quality—offering an unbeatable combination of a professional-grade rowing feel, near-indestructible construction, and precise performance tracking. While its comfort and storage scores are modest, its overall value proposition for dedicated athletes and fitness enthusiasts is unmatched. For those prioritizing interactive coaching or working within tight space or budget constraints, other models present compelling, albeit specialized, alternatives.

Methodology

This comparison employs a weighted decision matrix to objectively evaluate four leading rowing machines. Each model is assessed against six key criteria—Resistance Quality, Build Quality, Comfort, Monitor & Metrics, Storage, and Value for Money—which are assigned weights reflecting their relative importance for a full-body workout machine. Products receive a score out of 5 for each criterion, with detailed explanations justifying the rating. The final score is calculated by multiplying each rating by its weight and summing the results, providing a transparent, quantitative basis for comparison.

Why These Criteria Matter for a Rowing Machine

Selecting a rower goes beyond price and brand. The following factors determine whether a machine will deliver effective workouts, stand the test of time, and encourage consistent use.

  • Resistance Quality (Weight: 2): The heart of the rowing experience. A smooth, consistent resistance that mimics on-water rowing is essential for effective, joint-friendly workouts. The type—air, magnetic, electromagnetic, or water—affects noise, feel, and intensity progression.
  • Build Quality (Weight: 1.8): Durability dictates longevity and safety. A sturdy frame, solid rail construction, and a high weight capacity ensure the machine remains stable and reliable through intense, repeated use over many years.
  • Comfort (Weight: 1.6): If a machine is uncomfortable, it won’t be used. The seat, footrests, and handle ergonomics must accommodate different body types and allow for prolonged sessions without discomfort or strain.
  • Monitor & Metrics (Weight: 1.5): Feedback drives progress. A clear display that tracks key metrics (time, distance, stroke rate, calories) and, increasingly, connectivity for app integration or guided workouts, enhances motivation and training accuracy.
  • Storage (Weight: 1.4): Practicality for home use. Foldability, vertical storage capability, and overall footprint determine how easily the rower can be moved and stored, a critical factor in space-limited homes.
  • Value for Money (Weight: 1.5): The balance of cost against features, durability, and performance. The highest price doesn’t guarantee the best value, nor does the lowest price ensure a worthwhile investment.

Head-to-Head Rowing Machine Comparison

Concept2 Model D Rowing Machine

The Concept2 Model D is the professional benchmark, renowned for its unwavering reliability and performance in gyms and homes worldwide. It scored highest overall (40.10 pts) by dominating the most heavily weighted categories.

  • Performance & Build: It earned a perfect 5/5 in both Resistance Quality and Build Quality. Its air resistance system delivers an exceptionally smooth, dynamic drag that naturally increases with effort, creating an authentic rowing feel. The commercial-grade aluminum and stainless steel construction supports a 500 lb capacity and is built for decades of use with minimal maintenance.
  • Technology & Practicality: The PM5 monitor (5/5 for Metrics) sets the standard for accuracy, tracking detailed performance data and connecting via Bluetooth and ANT+ for use with popular rowing apps. Its main compromises are in Comfort (3/5), with a basic but functional seat and handle, and Storage (2/5), as its 96-inch length requires dedicated space, though it can be stored vertically.
  • Value Proposition: While priced at a premium ($990), its 4/5 Value for Money score reflects its unparalleled durability, high resale value, and lack of subscription fees. It’s an investment that pays off over the long term.

Best For: Serious athletes, frequent users, and anyone prioritizing durability and accurate performance metrics over high-tech screens or compact storage. Check Price on Amazon

Hydrow Wave Rowing Machine

The Hydrow Wave is a premium connected fitness rower designed for an immersive, tech-forward workout experience. It placed second (35.10 pts), scoring highly for its innovative approach but facing challenges in value and build.

  • Performance & Build: It shares a perfect 5/5 in Resistance Quality, thanks to a supremely quiet and smooth electromagnetic system. However, Build Quality is its weakest point at 3/5; while sturdy, its construction isn't as robust as the Concept2, with some users noting rail flex during aggressive rowing.
  • Technology & Practicality: The 22-inch HD touchscreen and live/on-demand class library earn a top score of 5/5 for Monitor & Metrics, providing engaging coaching and community features. Like the Concept2, it struggles with Storage (2/5), requiring significant floor space. Comfort is better at 4/5, with good ergonomics.
  • Value Proposition: Its low Value for Money score (2/5) stems from its high initial cost ($1,695) coupled with a mandatory monthly subscription to access its core interactive features, making it the most expensive long-term option.

Best For: Users who prioritize a quiet machine, immersive guided workouts, and modern design, and who are willing to pay a premium for a connected fitness ecosystem. Check Price on Amazon

NordicTrack RW900 Rowing Machine

The NordicTrack RW900 is a direct competitor to Hydrow, centering its value proposition on a large interactive screen and iFIT integration. It finished a close third (34.60 pts).

  • Performance & Build: It features very good Resistance Quality (4/5) with 26 digital magnetic resistance levels, though it doesn't quite match the natural feel of air or premium electromagnetic systems. Build Quality is adequate for home use (3/5) but not commercial-grade.
  • Technology & Practicality: Its standout feature is the 22-inch HD touchscreen with iFIT, earning a 5/5 for metrics and interactive programming. However, this feature also hinders Storage (2/5), as the large screen makes folding and vertical storage awkward. Comfort is rated 4/5.
  • Value Proposition: Valued at 3/5, its $1,599 price is largely tied to the screen and iFIT ecosystem. The rowing mechanism itself doesn't fully justify the cost, and the ongoing iFIT subscription adds to the total investment.

Best For: Users already invested in the iFIT ecosystem or those who want a large-screen, interactive training experience with automatic workout resistance adjustment. Check Price on Amazon

Sunny Health & Fitness SF-RW5515 Magnetic Rowing Machine

The Sunny Health & Fitness SF-RW5515 is the budget-friendly entry-point, offering basic functionality at a fraction of the cost. It ranked fourth (29.00 pts), excelling in affordability and storage.

  • Performance & Build: It demonstrates the trade-offs of its low price. Resistance Quality (3/5) is adequate and quiet but limited to 8 levels. Build Quality (2/5) is its most significant limitation, with a lower 250 lb weight capacity and less stable construction unsuitable for vigorous, long-term use.
  • Technology & Practicality: The monitor is extremely basic (2/5), showing only fundamental stats without connectivity. Its strengths are Storage (4/5), thanks to a foldable design, and Value for Money (4/5), given its accessible $249 price point.
  • Value Proposition: It represents a low-risk entry into rowing. The value is high for casual or beginner users but diminishes for anyone planning frequent, intense workouts due to durability concerns.

Best For: Beginners, casual users, those with severe budget constraints, or individuals needing a highly compact, storable machine for infrequent use. Check Price on Amazon

Final Verdict and Recommendations

The Concept2 Model D stands as the definitive choice for a home rowing machine. Its victory is anchored in superior performance where it matters most: the quality of the rowing stroke and the longevity of the machine. While it lacks the flashy screens of its smart competitors, it offers something more valuable: proven reliability, unmatched accuracy, and freedom from monthly fees. It is a tool designed purely for effective training, and it excels in that purpose.

However, the optimal choice depends on user priorities:

  • Choose the Concept2 Model D for unmatched durability, professional performance metrics, and the best long-term investment.
  • Choose the Hydrow Wave for a quiet, immersive, and visually engaging workout experience with live classes, accepting the higher total cost.
  • Choose the NordicTrack RW900 if you are dedicated to the iFIT training platform and want a large, interactive screen that controls your workout.
  • Choose the Sunny Health & Fitness SF-RW5515 as an introductory machine to gauge your interest in rowing without a major financial commitment, or for tight spaces.

For most individuals seeking a full-body workout machine that will remain a reliable fitness cornerstone for a decade or more, the Concept2 Model D’s combination of performance, quality, and honest value is impossible to beat.
