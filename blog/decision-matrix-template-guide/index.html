Free Decision Matrix Templates for Product Comparison

Starting a decision matrix from scratch takes time you might not have. That's why we've created free decision matrix templates for common decision types—from product comparisons to career choices. In this guide, we'll show you the best templates available and how to use them effectively.

Why Use a Decision Matrix Template?

Building a decision matrix requires two things: listing criteria and assigning weights. Templates solve both:

  • Pre-selected criteria: Experts have identified what factors matter most for common decisions
  • Default weights: Sensible starting points based on typical priorities
  • Faster setup: Jump straight to comparing options instead of building structure
  • Best practices: Learn what criteria others use for similar decisions

Of course, templates are starting points. You should customize criteria and weights to match your specific situation.

How to Customize Templates

Templates provide structure, but every decision is unique. Here's how to adapt them:

1. Review Each Criterion

Ask yourself: "Does this factor actually matter for MY decision?" Remove criteria that don't differentiate your options or that you don't care about.

2. Add Missing Criteria

Think about factors specific to your situation. Maybe "repair availability" matters if you're buying overseas, or "software included" is important for creative tools.

3. Adjust Weights

Default weights reflect typical priorities, but yours may differ. If you travel constantly, weight portability higher than a desk worker would.

4. Verify Total = 100%

After adjusting, make sure your weights still sum to 100%. Our tool does this automatically, but check if using spreadsheets.

Templates vs. Custom Matrices

When should you use a template versus building from scratch?

Use Templates When:

  • Making a common decision type (product comparisons)
  • You want a quick starting point
  • You're not sure what criteria to consider
  • Time is limited

Build from Scratch When:

  • Your decision is unique (career, relationships, locations)
  • Existing templates don't fit your category
  • You want complete control over structure
  • You're evaluating something highly specialized

Creating Your Own Templates

If you frequently make similar decisions, create reusable templates:

  1. Build a matrix for your first decision
  2. Score options and verify the structure works
  3. Export as a shareable link
  4. For future decisions, load that link and just update options/scores

This is particularly useful for recurring business decisions like vendor selection or hiring.
