Define Your Criteria

What factors matter in this decision? Add at least 2 criteria.

What are criteria?

Criteria are the factors that matter most in your decision. They're the standards you'll use to evaluate each option.

Examples: For choosing a laptop, criteria might include "Performance," "Battery Life," "Price," and "Build Quality."

Weight: Assign higher weights (1.0-3.0) to criteria that matter more. For example, if price is critical, give it a weight of 2.5.

Tip: Add 3-6 criteria for best results. Too few oversimplifies, too many creates analysis paralysis!

Add Your Alternatives

What options are you considering? Add at least 2 alternatives.

What are alternatives?

Alternatives are the different options you're choosing between. These are your potential choices.

Examples: If you're buying a laptop, your alternatives might be "MacBook Pro," "Dell XPS 15," "Lenovo ThinkPad," etc.

Details: Add a brief description and list the key pros and cons of each option to help guide your evaluation.

Tip: Include 2-5 realistic alternatives. More than that and decision-making becomes overwhelming!
