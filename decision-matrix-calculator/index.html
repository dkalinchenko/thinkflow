What is a Decision Matrix Calculator?

A decision matrix calculator is a quantitative tool that helps you make objective decisions

Weighted Scoring System

Unlike simple pro/con lists, a decision matrix calculator lets you assign different importance levels (weights) to each criterion. A factor that matters 40% to you will have twice the impact of one weighted at 20%.

Pugh Matrix Method

Based on the proven Pugh Matrix technique used by engineers and product managers, our calculator systematically compares alternatives against criteria to identify the best choice through mathematical scoring.

Remove Emotional Bias

Gut feelings can mislead you. By forcing you to define criteria upfront and score options systematically, a decision matrix calculator removes emotional bias and reveals the objectively best choice.

AI-Powered Suggestions

Visual Score Comparison

See results in clear charts and tables. Our calculator displays weighted scores, rankings, and strength/weakness breakdowns so you can understand exactly why one option scores higher.

How to Use Our Decision Matrix Calculator

Get objective results in 5 simple steps

1

Define Your Decision

Enter the decision you're making (e.g., "Which laptop should I buy?") and add the options you're considering. Start with 2-5 alternatives for best results.

2

Add Evaluation Criteria

List the factors that matter to your decision. For a laptop, this might include price, performance, battery life, display quality, and portability. Our AI can suggest criteria if you're unsure.

3

Assign Importance Weights

Distribute 100% across your criteria based on priority. If price matters twice as much as brand to you, give price 40% and brand 20%. This is where you personalize the decision matrix.

4

Score Each Option

Rate how well each alternative performs on each criterion using our 1-5 scale. Be consistent—a 4 should mean the same level of quality across all your ratings.

5

Review Your Results

The calculator multiplies each score by its weight and sums the results. The option with the highest weighted score is your objective best choice. Review the breakdown to understand why.

Decision Matrix Calculator FAQ

Common questions about weighted decision matrices

What's the difference between a decision matrix and a pros/cons list?

A pros/cons list treats all factors equally and doesn't quantify them. A decision matrix calculator assigns numerical weights to criteria and scores to options, producing an objective ranking rather than a subjective impression. This mathematical approach reveals the best choice even when gut feelings disagree.

How do I choose the right weights for my criteria?

Ask yourself: "If I could only optimize for one thing, what would it be?" That's your highest weight. Then consider trade-offs—would you sacrifice criterion A for more of criterion B? This helps you distribute weights that reflect your true priorities. Weights should total 100%.

Is this the same as a Pugh Matrix?

Similar but enhanced. A traditional Pugh Matrix compares options to a baseline using +/-/0 scores. Our decision matrix calculator adds weighted percentages and numerical scoring (1-5) for more granular comparison, plus AI suggestions and visual analytics.

Can I use this for team decisions?

Absolutely! Export your decision matrix as a shareable link. Team members can review your criteria, weights, and scores. For group decisions, you can have each person complete their own matrix and compare results to find consensus.

What if two options tie or score very close?

Close scores mean both options meet your criteria similarly. Consider adding more differentiating criteria, adjusting weights to reflect true priorities, or recognizing that either choice is valid. Sometimes a near-tie is valuable information.
